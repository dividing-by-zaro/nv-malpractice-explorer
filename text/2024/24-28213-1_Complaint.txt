COMPLAL

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through lan J. Cumings, Senior Deputy General Counsel and attorney for the IC,
having a reasonable basis to believe that Beverly Moemoe Yee, M.D. (Respondent) violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code
(NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating
the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a medical doctor holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 10658). Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on September 30, 2003

2. Patient A? was a forty-five (45) year-old male at the time of the events at issue.

3. Patient A established medical care with Respondent on March 26, 2018, due to the
retirement of his previous physician

4, Patient A was noted to be a habitual alcohol user in Respondents record on
March 26, 2018. Respondent diagnosed Patient A with panic attacks, anxiety, insomnia, and

5. During the March 26, 2018, visit, Respondent prescribed Patient A
benzodiazepines (Valium 10mg), despite its use being contraindicated with alcohol. Prior to
prescribing controlled substances to Patient A, Respondent failed to check Patient A’s Prescription
Monitoring Program (PMP) report or order drug tests Additionally, Respondent did not

substance.
6 On June 26, 2018, Patient A presented to Respondent for a follow-up appointment
Respondent noted Patient A’s continued alcohol abuse but refilled Patient A’s prescription for

benzodiazepines for another three (3) months. Respondent failed to check Patient A’s PMP report
or order any drug testing. Moreover, Respondent failed to document or discuss switching Patient
A from benzodiazepines to another anxiety medication, not contraindicated with alcohol use.

7. From September 2018, until November 2021, Respondent saw Patient A on no less
than nine (9) occasions. During each appointment, Respondent routinely failed to screen
Patient A for safety, and continued to prescribe Patient A benzodiazepines, despite his alcohol
abuse being noted on seven (7) of these nine (9) visits. Respondent documented Patient A’s
alcohol use as “chronic.” Respondent further failed to check Patient A’s PMP or order any drug
testing to determine if Patient A was abusing his medication, and did not address the underlying
anxiety order, or appropriately facilitate a referral to a psychologist for psychiatric treatment.
Throughout Respondent's treatment of Patient A, Respondent failed to obtain appropriate
informed written consent for the prescription of controlled substances.

10. NAC 630.040 defines malpractice as “the failure of a physician, in treating a
patient, to use the reasonable care, skill, or knowledge ordinarily used under similar
circumstances.”

11. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent failed
to use the reasonable care, skill or knowledge ordinarily used under similar circumstances when
rendering medical services to Patient A by inappropriately prescribing benzodiazepines to a
patient with chronic aleohol use. Furthermore, Respondent routinely failed to screen Patient A for
safety by running PMP reports to check if Patient A was being prescribed controlled substances by
other healthcare providers.

12. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT
NRS 630.3062(1)(a) - Failure to Maintain Proper Medical Records

13. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein

14 NRS 630.3062(1)(a) provides that the “failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate

and complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient” constitute

grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

15. Respondent failed to maintain proper medical records relating to the diagnosis,
treatment and care of Patient A, by failing to correctly document her actions when she treated
Patient A, whose medical records were not timely, legible, accurate, and complete as evidenced by
Respondent’s largely handwritten and illegible records which failed to adequately address Patient
A’s alcohol use, and proper informed consent.

16. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352

COUNTIN
NRS 630.306(1)(b)(2) - Violation of Standards of Practice Established by Regulation ~
Failure to Consult

17. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein

18. Violation of a standard of practice adopted by the Board is grounds for disciplinary
action pursuant to NRS 630.306(1)(b)(2).

19. NAC 630.210 requires a physician to “seck consultation with another provider of!
health care in doubtful or difficult cases whenever it appears that consultation may enhance the
quality of medical services.”

20. Respondent failed to timely seek consultation with regard to Patient A’s medical
condition from March 2018 through November 2021 and Respondent should have consulted with
an appropriate care provider to address the doubtfulness of the diagnosis of Patient A’s medical
condition and such a timely consultation would have confitmed or denied such a diagnosis and
may have enhanced the quality of medical care provided to the Patient with regard to Patient A’s
anxtety disorder and chromic alcohol usage Respondent failed to provide a proper referral nor
consult with a psychiatrist or psychologist in reference to Patient A’s anxiety disorder and chronic
alcohol use.

21. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent 1s subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

25. Pursuant to NAC 630.230(1)(k), a licensee shall not engage in the practice of|
writing prescriptions for controlled substances in a manner that deviates from the standards set
forth in the Model Policy

26. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent wrote
prescriptions to Patient A for benzodiazepines, a Schedule IV controlled substance, to treat in a
manner that deviated from the Model Policy by failing to continually pull Patient A’s PMP report
and require Patient A to submit to drug tests before prescribing benzodiazepines.

27. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNTY
NRS 630.306(1)(p) - Unsafe or Unprofessional Conduct

28. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

29. Engaging in any act that is unsafe or unprofessional conduct in accordance with
regulations adopted by the Board is grounds for disciplinary action against a licensee pursuant to
NRS 630.306(1)(p)..

30. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent wrote
prescriptions to Patient A for benzodiazepines with the knowledge Patient A used alcohol to
excess which is not consistent with the professional standards for the preseniption of controlled
substances nor the Dietary Guidelines

31. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.
