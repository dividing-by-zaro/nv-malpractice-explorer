COUNT
NRS 630.301(4) - Malpractice

12. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

13, NRS 630.301(4) provides that malpractice of a physician is grounds for initiating
disciplinary action against a licensee.

14. NAC 630.040 defines malpractice as “the failure of a physician, in treating a
patient, to use the reasonable care, skill, or knowledge ordinarily used under similar
circumstances.”

15. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent failed
to use the reasonable care, skill or knowledge ordinarily used under similar circumstances when
rendering medical services to Patient A by engaging in an inappropriate sexual relationship with

Patient A.

16. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as

provided in NRS 630.352.
COUNT
NRS 630.304(5) - Influencing a Patient to Engage in Sexual Activity

17. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

18. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent
influenced Patient A by utilizing knowledge gleaned in counseling sessions with Patient A to
coerce Patient A into an inappropriate sexual relationship with him.

19. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT IV
NRS 630.3062(1)(a) - Failure to Maintain Complete Medical Records

23. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

24. NRS 630.3062(1)(a) provides that the “failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate
and complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient” constitute
grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

25. Respondent failed to maintain complete medical records relating to the diagnosis,
treatment and care of Patient A, by failing to correctly document his actions when he treated
Patient A, whose medical records were not timely, legible, accurate, and complete as evidenced by
the lack of clinical notation on the March 9, 2022, counseling visit.

26. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

regulations adopted by the Board is grounds for disciplinary action against a licensee pursuant to
NRS 630.306(1)(p).

COUNT VIL
NRS 630.301(7) — Violation of Patient Trust and Exploitation of Physician and Patient
Relationship for Financial or Personal Gain

39. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein

40. NRS 630.301(7) provides that “engaging in conduct that violates the trust of a
