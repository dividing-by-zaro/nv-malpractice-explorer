COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Ian J. Cumings, Senior Deputy General Counsel and attorney for the IC,
having a reasonable basis to believe that Nilofar Kuraishi, M.D. (Respondent) violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code
(NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating
the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a medical doctor holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 6576). Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on August 1, 1992.

2: Patient A? was a seventy-eight (78) year-old male at the time of the events at issue.

3. Patient A is an elderly male with dementia who does not speak English.

4. On March 10, 2021, Patient A, along with his son as a translator, presented to
Respondent for a medical examination to complete an N-400 Application for Naturalization. The
'N-400 Application form is required to be completed for individuals seeking exemption to the

English and civics testing requirement due to physical or developmental disability or impairment.

5. Respondent examined Patient A and ordered laboratory studies but refused to fill
out Patient A’s N-400 Application form. Respondent documented Patient A as having significant
mental impairment, with memory loss and dementia, and assessed Patient A as having
hypertension and senility.

6. Patient A’s laboratory work was obtained on March 15, 2021, demonstrating
abnormalities in kidney function, hypercalcemia, uric acid, prostate specific antigen, and elevated
cholesterol. Despite significant laboratory findings, Respondent failed to document any clinical
plan for the management of Patient A’s conditions and did not follow-up with Patient A regarding
laboratory findings.

7. Patient A presented to Respondent on September 14, 2021, for an examination but
records show Patient A as having a negative review of symptoms. Despite this, Respondent
assessed Patient A as having hypertension, edema, weakness, weight loss, and chronic kidney
disease. Respondent further noted Patient A was seeing a neurologist, but did not document any
medical plan for treatment or discussion of previous laboratory results with Patient A.

COUNTI
NRS 630.301(4) - Malpractice
8. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by

reference as though fully set forth herein.

9. NRS 630.301(4) provides that malpractice of a physician is grounds for initiating
disciplinary action against a licensee.

10. NAC 630,040 defines malpractice as “the failure of a physician, in treating a
patient, to use the reasonable care, skill, or knowledge ordinarily used under similar
circumstances.”

11. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent failed
to use the reasonable care, skill or knowledge ordinarily used under similar circumstances when
rendering medical services to Patient A by failing to inform and appropriately manage Patient A’s
care, despite abnormal laboratory results and physical examination findings on March 10, 2021,

and September 14, 2021.

OUNT II
NRS 630.3062(1)(a) - Failure to Maintain Proper Medical Records

13. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

14. NRS 630,3062(1)(a) provides that the “failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate
and complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient” constitute
grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

15. Respondent failed to maintain complete medical records relating to the diagnosis,
treatment and care of Patient A, by failing to correctly document her actions when she treated
Patient A on March 10, 2021, whose medical records were not timely, legible, accurate, and
complete.

16. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.
