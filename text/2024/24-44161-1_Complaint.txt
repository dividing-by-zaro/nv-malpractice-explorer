‘The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners

the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

5. On November 12, 2018, the urinalysis demonstrated sixteen (16) white blood cells,

6. A white blood cell count over five (5) in urine is typically abnormal and indicative

urinary tract infection.

8. Patient A was discharged on November 12, 2018, at approximately 10:30 a.m.,
with instructions to follow-up with a urologist.

9. During Patient A’s stay at the emergency department on November 12, 2018,
Respondent did not document a urinary tract infection or administer or prescribe antibiotics to
Patient A.

10. Patient A presented again to the emergency department at Summerlin Hospital on
November 14, 2018, and was diagnosed with severe sepsis resulting from a urinary tract infection.

11. Due to the infection, Patient A developed severe medical complications resulting in
a lengthy hospitalization.

12. Additionally, the urine culture from November 12, 2018, was reported on
November 14, 2018, demonstrating an abnormal amount of Escherichia coli in Patient A’s urine.

NRS 630.301(4) - Malpractice

reference as though fully set forth herein.

15. | NAC 630.040 defines malpractice as “the failure of a physician, in treating a
patient, to use the reasonable care, skill, or knowledge ordinarily used under similar
circumstances.”

16. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent failed
to use the reasonable care, skill or knowledge ordinarily used under similar circumstances when
rendering medical services to Patient A, by failing to diagnose and treat Patient A’s unary tract
infection in the setting of an obstructed ureteral stone, including by failing to administer or
prescribe antibiotics to Patient A.

17. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT II
NRS 630.3062(1)(a) - Failure to Maintain Appropriate Medical Records

18. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

19. NRS 630.3062(1)(a) provides that the “failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate
and complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient” constitute
grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

20. Respondent failed to maintain timely, legible, accurate and complete medical
records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of Patient A, by failing to accurately note
Patient A’s urinary tract infection on November 12, 2018.

21. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.
