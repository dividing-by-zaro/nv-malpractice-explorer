COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Alexander J. Hinman, Deputy General Counsel, and attorney for the IC,
having a reasonable basis to believe that Allison Amy Budzban, CRT, (Respondent) violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code
(NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating
the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

I. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a Respiratory Therapist
holding an active license to practice respiratory therapy in the State of Nevada
(License No RC3400). Respondent was originally licensed by the Board on December 31, 2020

2. At all times relative to this Complaint, Respondent was a Certified Respiratory
Therapist (CRT).

3. In April of 2022, Respondent informed her employer that she was certified as a
Registered Respiratory Therapist (RRT), when she had not yet received a passing score on the
Clinical Setting Examination, which is required to obtain an RRT certification.

4. On December 8, 2022, the National Board for Respiratory Care (NBRC) met and

conducted a hearing regarding Respondent’s inclusion of misinformation in an NBRC credential

Complaint was authorized for filing, was composed of Board members Bret W. Frey, M.D., Col. Eric D. Wade,
