COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Ian J. Cumings, Senior Deputy General Counsel and attorney for the IC,
having a reasonable basis to believe that Abdollah Assad, M.D. (Respondent) violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code
(NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating
the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a medical doctor holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 10883). Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on April 8, 2004.

2. Patient A? was a thirty-one (31) year-old female at the time of the events at issue.

3. During the relevant time period, Patient A was a long-established psychiatric
patient of Respondent, having regular visits being under Respondent's care and treatment since
June 23, 2011. During these regular visits, Respondent documented a history of anxiety and
problematic relationships that included her difficult relationship with her boyfriend on repeated

occasions. Respondent's records from these visits are handwritten and largely illegible.

4. On September 28, 2020, Respondent documented a telephone visit with Patient A
who informed Respondent that she was moving out of the state. During this telephone visit,
Respondent prescribed Latuda, Xanax, and Adderall for her

5. On or about October 12, 2020, Respondent was contacted by Patient A who was
staying in a motel and admitted to having attempted suicide. Respondent did not instruct
Patient A to go to an emergency room, call an ambulance, or call police but instead came to the
motel and picked up Patient A.

6. Respondent did not take Patient A to the hospital, nor attempt to initiate a legal
2000 mandatory hold to observe Patient A after her attempted suicide. Instead, Respondent took
Patient A to another motel, and engaged in sexual intercourse with her, despite his awareness of|
troublesome relationships in the past and her obvious fragile state.

7. On October 16, 2020, following the sexual encounter, Respondent continued to
prescribe Adderall to Patient A, and documented that Patient A was going to seck a new
physician. Respondent did not refer Patient A to a new psychiatrist or make arrangements for the
transfer of her care.

COUNTI
NRS 630.301(4) - Malpractice

8. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

». NRS 630.301(4) provides that malpractice of a physician is grounds for initiating
disciplinary action against a licensee.

10. NAC 630,040 defines malpractice as “the failure of a physician, in treating a
patient, to use the reasonable care, skill, or knowledge ordinarily used under similar
circumstances.”

11. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent failed
to use the reasonable care, skill or knowledge ordinarily used under similar circumstances when

rendering medical services to Patient A by engaging in sexual intercourse and having an

COUNT
NRS 630.304(5) — Influencing a Patient to Engage in Sexual Acti

ity
13. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by

reference as though fully set forth herein.

14. NRS 630.304(5) provides that influencing a patient in order to engage in sexual
activity with the patient or others is grounds for initiating disciplinary action.

15. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent
influenced Patient A by failing to properly admit her to an appropriate care facility or get
Patient A emergency assistance following a suicide attempt despite her documented history of
troubled relationships, but instead took Patient A to a motel and engaged in sexual intercourse
with her.

16. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT It
NRS 630.301(5) — Engaging in Sexual Activity with a Patient

17. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

18. NRS 630.301(5) provides that engaging by a practitioner in any sexual activity
with a patient who is currently being treated by the practitioner constitutes grounds for initiating
disciplinary action.

19. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent
engaged in sexual intercourse with Patient A and subsequently continued to prescribe controlled
substances to her as her physician.

COUNT IV
NRS 630.306(1)(p) - Unsafe or Unprofessional Conduct

21. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

22. Engaging in any act that is unsafe or unprofessional conduct in accordance with
regulations adopted by the Board is grounds for disciplinary action against a licensee pursuant to
NRS 630.306(1)(p).

23. — As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent’s
conduct was unprofessional and violated the physician-patient relationship when Respondent
engaged in sexual intercourse with Patient A, a vulnerable psychiatric patient.

24. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT V
NRS 630,304(7) - Terminating Medical Care without Adequate Notice to a Patient

25. __Alll of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

26. | NRS 630.304(7) provides that terminating the medical care of a patient without
adequate notice or without making other arrangements for the continued care of the patient is
grounds for initiating disciplinary action.

27. Respondent terminated the medical care of Patient A without adequate notice when
he discontinued care of Patient A on October 16, 2020. Respondent noted Patient A’s intention to
seek another provider but failed to coordinate her care or provide a referral to an appropriate
physician following his October 16, 2020, prescription of Adderall.

28. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as

provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT VI
NRS 630.301(7) - Violation of Patient Trust and Exploitation of Physi

in and Patient

Relationship for Financial or Personal Gain

29. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

30. NRS 630.301(7) provides that “engaging in conduct that violates the trust of a
patient and exploits the relationship between the physician and the patient for financial or other
personal gain” is grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

31. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent
violated the trust of a patient and exploited the relationship between the Respondent and Patient A
by gaining intimate knowledge of Patient A during psychiatric treatment, and subsequently
engaging in a sexual relationship with Patient A while she was in a vulnerable state.

32. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.
