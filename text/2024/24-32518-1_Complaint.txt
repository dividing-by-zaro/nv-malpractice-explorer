COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through William P. Shogren, Deputy General Counsel and attorney for the IC,
having a reasonable basis to believe that Stephen Brent Horsley, M.D. (Respondent) violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code
(NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating
the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a medical doctor holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 12245). Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on April 18, 2007.

2. Patient A? was a fifty-seven (57) year-old female at the time of the events at issue.

3. Patient A presented to Respondent on September 18, 2020, for evaluation of an
enlarged right groin node that had previously been documented on an ultrasound. Respondent
planned to perform an excision of Patient A’s right groin lymph node.

4. On October 26, 2020, Respondent performed the excision, and then sent the

specimen for a biopsy to a pathology services company.

5. On or about October 30, 2020, Respondent received a preliminary pathology report
from the pathologist. The report stated, in pertinent part, “worrisome for B-cell lymphoma with
follicular pattern however bcl-2 strain is not confirmatory. Additional ancillary studies are
pending for further evaluation . . . an addendum final report will follow.” The report also noted
that the node would be sent for outside consultation for future findings.

6. On or about November 13, 2020, another pathologist sent an addendum to the
pathology report to Respondent, stating that the tests were positive for B-cell rearrangement and
that the results confirmed a diagnosis of lymphoma.

7. Patient A met with Respondent on November 17, 2020, for a post-operative visit.
Respondent’s notes from this visit stated, “path pending.” Respondent’s notes from the visit did
not indicate that Respondent informed Patient A of the equivocal nature of the biopsy or the
probability of lymphoma.

8. Respondent did not schedule a follow-up visit with Patient A after
November 17, 2020, nor did Respondent instruct Patient A to consult with an oncologist.

9. On or about November 19, 2020, another pathologist sent to Respondent a further
addendum to the pathology report. This addendum made the diagnosis of Follicular Lymphoma.
Respondent did not directly convey the results of the November 19, 2020, addendum to Patient A.

COUNT I
NRS 630.301(4) - Malpractice

10. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

11. NRS 630.301(4) provides that malpractice of a physician is grounds for initiating
disciplinary action against a licensee.

12. NAC 630.040 defines malpractice as “the failure of a physician, in treating a
patient, to use the reasonable care, skill, or knowledge ordinarily used under similar
circumstances.”

13. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent failed

to use the reasonable care, skill or knowledge ordinarily used under similar circumstances when

rendering medical services to Patient A, by failing to inform her on or after
November 17, 2020, of the equivocal nature of the October 30, 2020, preliminary pathology report
or the probability of lymphoma. Respondent further failed to use the reasonable care, skill or

Patient A, by failing to inform Patient A of further pathology report findings after Patient A’s visit

14. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as

COUNT II
NRS 630.3062(1)(a) - Failure to Maintain Appropriate Medical Records

15. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

16. | NRS 630.3062(1)(a) provides that the “failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate
and complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient” constitute
grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

17. Respondent failed to maintain timely, legible, accurate and complete medical
records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of Patient A, by failing to accurately note any
discussions with Patient A regarding the equivocal nature of the October 30, 2020, preliminary
pathology report or the probability of lymphoma.

18. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.
