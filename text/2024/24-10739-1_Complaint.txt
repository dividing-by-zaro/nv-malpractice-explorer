COMPLAIN’

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through William P. Shogren, Deputy General Counsel and attorney for the IC,
having a reasonable basis to believe that David Paul Gorezyca, M.D. (Respondent) violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code
(NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating
the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a medical doctor holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 7360). Respondent is
Board-Certified in Diagnostic Radiology by the American Board of Radiology. Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on February 23, 1995.

2. Patient A? was a twenty-five (25) year-old female at the time of the events at issue.

a Patient A presented to the emergency department at Sunrise Hospital on
October 29, 2019, with complaints of persistent chest pain, shortness of breath, and a headache
Patient A’s medical notes from Sunrise Hospital on this date also indicated a personal history of

pulmonary embolism.

4. On October 30, 2019, Patient A underwent a computed tomography angiography
(CTA) scan of her chest with contrast.

5. The CTA scan taken on October 30, 2019, clearly demonstrated a pulmonary
embolism.

6. On October 30, 2019, Respondent interpreted and electronically signed a CT scan
study of Patient A and recorded his impression as “no evidence for pulmonary thromboembolism”
and “no acute disease.”

7. Patient A was later discharged from the emergency department at Sunrise Hospital
on October 30, 2019. Patient A received discharge instructions from the emergency department
for “Chest Pain of unclear etiology.”

8. Patient A was readmitted to Sunrise Hospital on November 1, 2019, whereafter she
developed bradycardia and lost pulses. Patient A passed away on November 1, 2019.

COUNTI
NRS 630.301(4) - Malpractice

9. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein,

10. NRS 630.301(4) provides that malpractice of a physician is grounds for initiating
disciplinary action against a licensee.

11. NAC 630.040 defines malpractice as “the failure of a physician, in treating a
patient, to use the reasonable care, skill, or knowledge ordinarily used under similar
circumstances.”

12. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent failed
to use the reasonable care, skill or knowledge ordinarily used under similar circumstances when
rendering medical services to Patient A, by failing to accurately identify and note Patient A’s
pulmonary embolism on the October 30, 2019, CTA scan.

13. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT I
NRS 630.3062(1)(a) - Failure to Maintain Appropriate Medical Records

14. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
teference as though fully set forth herein.

15. NRS 630.3062(1)(a) provides that the “failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate
and complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient” constitute
grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

16. Respondent failed to maintain timely, legible, accurate and complete medical
records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of Patient A, by failing to accurately note
Patient A’s pulmonary embolism on the October 30, 2019, CTA scan.

17. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.
