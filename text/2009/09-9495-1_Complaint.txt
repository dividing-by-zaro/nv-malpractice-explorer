COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, composed
of Charles N. Held, M.D., Chairman, Jean Stoess, M.A., Member, and Benjamin Rodriguez, M.D.,
Member, by and through Lyn E. Beggs, General Counsel for the Nevada State Board of Medical
Examiners, having a reasonable basis to believe that Charles Mahakian, M.D., hereinafter referred
to as Dr. Mahakian, has violated the provisions of NRS Chapter 630, hereby issues its formal
Complaint, stating the Investigative Committee's charges and allegations, as follows:

1, Dr. Mahakian is currently licensed in active status, and was so licensed by the
Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, hereinafter referred to as “the Board,” on
September 13, 1986 (License No. 5441), pursuant to the provisions of Chapter 630 of the Nevada
Revised Statutes, and at all times addressed herein was so licensed.

2. Person A was a thirty-five year old female at the beginning of the matter in
question. Her true identity is not disclosed to protect her privacy, but has been made known to
Dr. Mahakian.

3. Person A was hired by Dr. Mahakian as a part time employee in his office. During
the course of her employment, Person A asked Dr. Mahakian to refill a prescription of Prozac for her
which Dr. Mahakian proceeded to do.

4, Subsequently, between July 2006 and August 2008, Dr. Mahakian proceeded to
prescribe multiple controlled substances for Person A including, but not limited to, Clonazepam,
Ambien, Zolpidem, Lortab, Alprazolam and Temazepam as indicated by a Patient Drug Utilization
Report for Person A.

5. Person A was not a patient of Dr. Mahakian and he kept no medical records
documenting any diagnosis, treatment or care of Person A during the time in which Dr. Mahakian
was prescribing controlled substances for her.

Count I

6. Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.306(2)(c) provides that engaging in conduct
which is in violation of a regulation adopted by the State Board of Pharmacy is grounds for
initiating disciplinary action against a licensee.

7. Nevada Administrative Code Section 639.945(1)(0) prohibits the prescribing of a
drug as a prescribing practitioner to a patient with whom the prescribing practitioner does not have
a bona fide therapeutic relationship.

8. Dr. Mahakian violated NRS 630.306(2)(c) when he prescribed multiple
medications to Person A, an individual with whom he did not have a bona fide therapeutic
relationship.

9. By reason of the foregoing, Dr. Mahakian is subject to discipline by the Nevada
State Board of Medical Examiners as provided in Section 630.352 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

Count II

10. Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.3062(1) provides that failure to maintain
timely, legible, accurate and complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care
of a patient is grounds for initiating disciplinary action against a licensee.

11. Dr. Mahakian kept no medical records regarding any diagnosis, treatment or care of
Person A although he prescribed multiple controlled substances for Person A between
July 2006 and August 2008 as indicated by a Patient Drug Utilization report for Person A.

12. By reason of the foregoing, Dr. Mahakian is subject to discipline by the Nevada

State Board of Medical Examiners as provided in Section 630.352 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.
