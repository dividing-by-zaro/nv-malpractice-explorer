COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, composed
of S. Daniel McBride, M.D. and Vani Heffner,. Member, having a reasonable basis to believe that
Kent Skogerson, M.D., hereinafter referred to as Dr. Skogerson, has violated the provisions of NRS
Chapter 630, hereby issues its formal Complaint, stating the Investigative Committee's charges and
allegations, as follows:

l. Dr. Skogerson is currently licensed in suspended for non-payment status, but was
licensed in active status at the time of all events discussed herein and was so licensed by the Nevada
State Board of Medical Examiners, on September 17, 1988 (License No. 5737), pursuant to the
provisions of Chapter 630 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

2. Patient A was a twenty-seven year old female .when she first presented tq
Dr. Skogerson. Her true identify is not disclosed to protect her privacy, but her identity is disclosed
in the Patient Designation served on Dr. Skogerson along with a copy of this Complaint.

3. Patient A first presented to Dr. Skogerson in July 2002 to discuss surgical weight
loss alternatives. It was determined that Patient A would undergo a Roux-en-Y gastric bypass
procedure after completion of required consultations.

4, On January 16, 2003, Patient A saw Dr. Skogerson for a pre-operative history and

physical for the Roux-en-Y gastric bypass procedure which was scheduled for January 23, 2003}

Patient A’s vitals at the time indicated blood pressure of 120/92, pulse of 84, respiration of 12 and
oxygen saturation on room air at 96%.
5. Patient A was admitted to Carson Tahoe Hospital and underwent a Roux-en-Y
gastric bypass surgery performed by Dr. Skogerson on January 23, 2003, a JP drain was placed and
she was discharged to recovery in satisfactory condition. .
6. Patient A recovered in the hospital for the next several days. During her recovery
Patient A had consistent tachycardia, had oxygen saturation levels in the low 90% range even on
supplemental oxygen, and she was slightly febrile throughout her stay at Carson Tahoe Hospital.
7. Patient A had blood work completed on January 26, 2003 which indicated a white
blood cell count of 1589/uL.
8. Patient A was seen by Dr. Skogerson on the morning of January 27, 2003 and was
subsequently discharged home with supplemental oxygen.
9. On January 30, 2003, Patient A saw Dr. Skogerson in his office for a post-operative
visit. During the visit Patient A’s pulse was 128 and her oxygen saturation was 93% on room air
Dr. Skogerson notes indicate that her drain was cloudy and smelly, indicating a leak. Patient A wa
sent to get a CT scan and an x-ray of her chest and abdomen. Blood work was also completed
which indicated a WBC of almost 19,000/uL.
10. Patient A was ultimately discovered to have a gastric leak, a known complication of
the Roux-en-Y gastric bypass procedure, and was appropriately treated.
Count I
11. Nevada Administrative Code Section 630.040 defines malpractice as the failure of 4
physician, in treating a patient, to use the reasonable care, skill, or knowledge ordinarily used under
similar circumstances.
12. Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.301(4) provides that malpractice is grounds for
initiating disciplinary action against a licensee.
13. Dr. Skogerson failed to use the reasonable care, skill, or knowledge ordinarily used
under similar circumstances when he discharged Patient A from the hospital when she was not

stable for discharge as evidenced by the totality of her condition at the time of discharge and
