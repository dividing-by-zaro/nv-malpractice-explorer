COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee of the Board of Medical Examiners of the state of Nevada,
composed of Charles N. Held, M.D., Chairman, Benjamin Rodriguez, M.D., Member, and
Jean Stoess, M.A., Member, by and through Lyn E. Beggs, General Counsel for the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners, having a reasonable basis to believe that Aaron Jay Goodrum, M.D.,
has violated the provisions of NRS Chapter 630, hereby issues its formal Complaint, stating the
Investigative Committee's charges and allegations, as follows:

1. Dr. Goodrum is currently licensed in active status, and was so licensed by the
Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, hereinafter referred to as “the Board,” on
June 6, 2006 (License No. 11874), pursuant to the provisions of Chapter 630 of the Nevada
Revised Statutes, and at all times addressed herein was so licensed.

Count I

2. Dr. Goodrum was issued a medical license by the State of Florida, Department of
Health in 2005.

3. On March 3, 2008, the State of Florida, Department of Health filed an
administrative disciplinary complaint against Dr. Goodrum alleging that in September 2007,
Dr. Goodrum had pled guilty to false imprisonment and battery, felony charges, in Palm Beach
County, Florida.

4, On June 19, 2008, the Board of Medicine in Florida issued a final order adopting a
settlement agreement to resolve the outstanding administrative complaint against Dr. Goodrum
which included a reprimand, a fine, costs, and additional terms and conditions,

5. Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.301(3) provides that any disciplinary action
regarding a license to practice any type of medicine taken by another state or any other jurisdiction
is grounds for discipline.

6. The disciplinary action taken by the State of Florida, Department of Health
constitutes a violation of the provisions of NRS 630.301(3).

7. By reason of the foregoing, Dr. Goodrum is subject to discipline by the Nevada
State Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NRS 630.352.

Count I

8. Dr. Goodrum was issued a medical license by the state of Missouri in July 2001.

9. On January 16, 2009, the Missouri State Board of Registration for the Healing Arts
held a hearing on a disciplinary complaint based upon Dr. Goodrum’s guilty plea to felony false
imprisonment in the state of Florida in September 2007. After deliberations, the Missouri Board
revoked Dr. Goodrum’s license to practice medicine in the state of Missouri. An order was
entered to this effect on January 23, 2009.

10. Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.301(3) provides that any disciplinary action
regarding a license to practice any type of medicine taken by another state or any other jurisdiction
is grounds for discipline.

11. The disciplinary action taken by the Missouri State Board of Registration for the
Healing Arts constitutes a violation of the provisions of NRS 630.301(3).

12. By reason of the foregoing, Dr. Goodrum is subject to discipline by the Nevada
State Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NRS 630.352.
