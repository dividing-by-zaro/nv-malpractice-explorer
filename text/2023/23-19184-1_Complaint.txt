COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Ian J. Cumings, Deputy General Counsel and attorney for the IC, having
a reasonable basis to believe that Nader Hossein Beheshti, M.D. (Respondent) violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code
(NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating
the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a medical doctor holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 9410). Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on June 9, 2000. Respondent's specialty is listed as radiology,
diagnostic radiology, and nuclear radiology.

2. Patient A? was a fifty-seven (57) year-old female at the time of the events at issue.

3. On September 27, 2018, Patient A presented to St. Rose Dominican Hospital, Siena
Campus (St. Rose Hospital) emergency room via an ambulance, following a fall from a retaining
wall, landing face first

4. Patient A had multiple radiological images taken at the hospital, including a CT
scan of maxillofacial structures, head, and neck.

5. Respondent was the radiologist who read and interpreted Patient A’s diagnostic
imaging on September 27, 2018, and noted Patient A’s relevant medical history including
posttraumatic facial pain.

6. After reading the diagnostic testing results, Respondent determined there were no
acute fractures or dislocations and reported this in Patient A’s medical records. However,
Respondent failed to identify a comminuted fracture of the right condylar head of the mandible,
which was clearly present on Patient A’s CT imaging studies.

7. Respondent'’s failure to appropriately diagnosis Patient A’s fracture, which led to a
delay in diagnosis and proper treatment.

COUNTI
NRS 630.301(4) - Malpractice

8. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein

9. NRS 630.301(4) provides that malpractice of a physician is grounds for initiating
disciplinary action against a licensee.

10. NAC 630.040 defines malpractice as “the failure of a physician, in treating a
patient, to use the reasonable care, skill, or knowledge ordinarily used under similar
circumstances.”

11. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent failed
to use the reasonable care, skill or knowledge ordinarily used under similar circumstances when
rendering medical services to Patient A, by failing to identify Patient A’s comminuted fracture of
the right condylar head of the mandible, leading to a delay in diagnosis and treatment.

12. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352
