COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Donald K. White, Senior Deputy General Counsel and attomey for the IC,
having a reasonable basis to believe that Michael Scott Mall, M.D. (Respondent) violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC)
Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating the IC’s
charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a medical doctor holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 6074). Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on July 1, 1990.

2. Patient A? was a thirty-eight (38) year-old female at the tme of the events at issue.

3. Patient A first presented to Respondent on or about March 22, 2017, for laser hair
removal of the bikini/Brazilian area.

4. On or about October 13, 2017, Patient A signed a consent form for multiple
treatments and received her first treatment that day for the bikini/Brazilian area.

5. Over approximately the next two (2) years, Patient A received eleven (11)
treatments for laser hair removal in the bikini/Brazilian area, seven (7) treatments to the upper
legs, and seven (7) treatments to the lower legs.

6. On Patient A’s last treatment, on or about September 6, 2019, Patient A quickly
developed blisters on the front of the left lower leg and the bikini area after laser treatment
performed by Respondent.

7. Respondent applied a topical ointment, wrapped the area, and scheduled a follow-
up appointment in three (3) days. Patient A did not return for that appointment and was no longer
treated by Respondent.

8. In his medical records and notes for this visit, Respondent failed to document his
interactions with Patient A, the laser settings and intensity used during the treatment, the pulse
duration, or what areas he treated.

COUNTI
NRS 630.301(4) - Malpractice
9. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by

reference as though fully set forth herein.

10. NRS 630.301(4) provides that malpractice of a physician is grounds for initiating
disciplinary action against a licensee.

11. NAC 630.040 defines malpractice as “the failure of a physician, in treating a
patient, to use the reasonable care, skill, or knowledge ordinarily used under similar
circumstances.”

12. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent failed
to use the reasonable care, skill or knowledge ordinarily used under similar circumstances when
rendering medical services to Patient A when he and his medical assistants failed to accurately and
completely document laser settings, pulse duration, and areas treated, making it extremely difficult
to provide safe and consistent treatments for Patient A.

13. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as

provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT II
NRS 630.3062(1)(a) - Failure to Maintain Proper Medical Records
14, All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein,
15. NRS 630.3062(1)(a) provides that the “failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate

and complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient” constitute

ting discipline against a licensee.

16. Respondent failed to maintain accurate and complete medical records relating to
the diagnosis, treatment and care of Patient A, by failing to correctly document laser settings,
pulse duration, and areas treated, making it extremely difficult to provide safe and consistent
treatments for Patient A.

17. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.
