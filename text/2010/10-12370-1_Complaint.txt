The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, composed
at the time filing was approved of Charles N. Held, M.D., Theodore B. Berndt, M.D. and
Ms. Renee West, having a reasonable basis to believe that Antonino Gumina, M.D., hereinafter
referred to as Dr. Gumina, has violated the provisions of NRS Chapter 630, hereby issues its
formal Complaint, stating the Investigative Committee's charges and allegations, as follows:

1. Dr. Gumina is currently licensed in active status (License No. 9897), and has been
so licensed since July 18, 2001 by the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners pursuant to the
provisions of Chapter 630 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

2. Patient A was a twenty-four year old woman at the time she began to be treated by
Dr. Gumina for Graves Disease. Her true identity is not disclosed to protect her privacy, but her
identity is disclosed in the Patient Designation served on Dr. Gumina along with a copy of this
Complaint.

3. Patient A was seen by Dr. Gumina in 2005 and 2006 but began treatment with him
for Graves Disease in late 2008.

4, Sometime in early 2009, Dr. Gumina began to have a relationship with Patient A of
a sexual nature. Dr. Gumina continued to see and treat Patient A as a patient after beginning this

relationship.

5. On June 15, 2009, an ultrasound showed Patient A to be approximately eight weeks
pregnant.

6. In August, 2009, in correspondence to the Board, Dr. Gumina admitted to being in
a relationship with Patient A and further admitted to being the father of her unborn child. Medical
records indicate that Dr. Gumina was continuing to see Patient A as a patient in July 2009.

Count I

7. Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.301(5) provides that the engaging by a
practitioner in any sexual activity with a patient who is currently being treated by the practitioner
is grounds for initiating disciplinary action against a licensee.

8. Dr. Gumina began, and continued having, a sexual relationship with Patient A
while she was being seen and treated by him as a patient and accordingly he has violated Section
630.301(5) of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

9. By reason of the foregoing, Dr. Gumina is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in Section 630.352 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

1. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners give Dr. Gumina notice of the
charges herein against him and give him notice that he may file an answer to the Complaint herein
as set forth in Section 630.339 of the Nevada Revised Statutes within twenty (20) days of service
of the Complaint.

2. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners set a time and place for a
formal hearing after holding an Early Case Conference pursuant to NRS 630.339(3);

3. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners determine what sanctions it
determines to impose if it determines there has been a violation or violations of the Medical
Practice Act (Nevada Revised Statutes Chapter 630) committed by Dr. Gumina; and

4, That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners make, issue and serve on
Dr. Gumina its findings of facts, conclusions of law and order, in writing, that includes the
sanctions imposed; and
