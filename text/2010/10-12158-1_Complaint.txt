COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners (Board),
composed of Charles N. Held, M.D., Chairman, Theodore B. Berndt, M.D., Member, and
Valerie J. Clark, BSN, RHU, LUTCF, Member, at the time of the authorization of filing this formal
complaint, by and through Edward O. Cousineau, Deputy Executive Director for the Board and
counsel for the Investigative Committee, having a reasonable basis to believe that
Abdul-Sami Siddiqui, M.D., hereinafter referred to as "Respondent," has violated the provisions of
Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630, hereby issues its formal Complaint, stating the
Investigative Committee's charges and allegations, as follows:

1. Respondent license to practice medicine is currently active, and at all times
alleged herein, Respondent was licensed by the Board, pursuant to the provisions of Chapter 630
of the NRS.

2. In November of 2009, Respondent accepted the entry of an order by the Texas
Medical Board which imposed an administrative penalty against him in the amount of $4,000.00.
The administrative penalty was based upon Respondent’s submission of false or misleading
statement on two applications for licensure in the state of Texas, more specifically, Respondent’s

failure to report an arrest for domestic battery which occurred in 1996. (See Exhibit 1)

3. Respondent failed to subsequently notice the Board of the agreed violation of
Texas law and the associated administrative penalty imposed against him.

4. Section 630.301(3) of the NRS provides that any disciplinary action, including
without limitation, the revocation, suspension, modification or limitation of the license to practice
any type of medicine by any other jurisdiction is grounds for disciplinary action.

5. The disciplinary action related to Respondent’s license to practice medicine in the
state of Texas, constitutes a violation of NRS 630.301(3).

6. Section 630.306(11) of the NRS provides that the failure by a licensee to report in
writing, within 30 days, any disciplinary action taken against the licensee by another state, is
grounds for disciplinary action.

7. The failure of Respondent to report to the Board the disciplinary action, in the
state of Texas, constitutes a violation of NRS 630.306(11).

8. Based upon the forgoing, Respondent has violated NRS 630.301(3) and
NRS 630.306(11), and is subject to discipline by the Board as provided NRS 630.352.

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

1. That the Board fix a time and place for a formal hearing;

2. That the Board give Respondent notice of the charges herein against him, the time
and place set for the hearing, and the possible sanctions against him;

3. That the Board determine what sanctions it determines to impose for the violation or
violations committed by Respondent; and

4, That the Board make, issue and serve on Respondent its findings of facts,
conclusions of law and order, in writing, that includes the sanctions imposed.

ORDER IMPOSING
ADMINISTRATIVE PENALTY

| ABDUL SIDDIQUI, M.D.


Applicant for

Texas Medical License |

The Texas Medical Board (TMB) has determined that Abdul Siddiqui, M.D. (“the Respondent”)
is in violation of Texas Occupations Code §164.052(a)(1), based on the submission of a false or
misleading statement in two applications for full licensure regarding his failure to report his 1996
arrest for domestic battery that was subsequently dismissed with no action taken. TMB hereby
grants Respondent’s application for a license and imposes an administrative penalty for this
violation, as authorized by Section 165.001, ‘fexas Occupations Code and Board rules §§187.75-
82.

The amount of the imposed penalty is $4,000.00. Payment of this penalty is due no later than 60
days after the Respondent receives notice of this Order. The administrative penalty shall be paid
in a single payment by cashier's check or money order payable to the Texas Medical Board and
shall be submitted to the Executive Director of the Board for routing so as to be remitted to the
Comptroller of Texas for deposit in the general revenue fund. Respondent's failure to pay the
administrative penalty as ordered shall constitute grounds for further disciplinary action by the
Board, and may result in a referral by the Executive Director of the Board for collection by the
Office of the Attorney General.
