COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, composed
at the time filing was approved of Charles N. Held, M.D., Theodore B. Berndt, M.D. and
Ms. Valerie Clark, BSN, RHU, LUTCF, having a reasonable basis to believe that
Thomas Sazani, M.D., hereinafter referred to as Dr. Sazani, has violated the provisions of
NRS Chapter 630, hereby issues its formal Complaint, stating the Investigative Committee's
charges and allegations, as follows:

1. Dr. Sazani is currently licensed in active status (License No. 5890), and was
originally licensed on July 1, 1989 by the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners pursuant to
the provisions of Chapter 630 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

2. Dr. Sazani currently holds a license to practice medicine in the state of California
and was so licensed on December 16, 1995.

3. On June 1, 2009, the Medical Board of California served an Accusation on
Dr. Sazani alleging three counts of violations of the California Business and Professions Code,
related to the prescribing of a medication over the internet without performing any physical
examination of the patient.

4. Dr. Sazani, with the advice of counsel, entered into a settlement agreement with the
Medical Board of California in November 2009. The Stipulated Settlement and Disciplinary
Order revoked Dr. Sazaniâ€™s medical license in California, with the revocation being stayed and
Dr. Sazani being placed on three years probation, subject to various terms and conditions.

5. On January 13, 2010, the Medical Board of California issued its Decision adopting
the Stipulated Settlement and Disciplinary Order which became effective on February 11, 2010.

6. Dr. Sazani notified the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners in a timely
fashion of the disciplinary action taken by the Medical Board of California.

| Count I

7. NRS 630.301(3) provides that any disciplinary action, including the revocation,
suspension, modification or limitation of a license to practice any type of medicine taken by
another state is grounds for initiating disciplinary action against a licensee.

8. Dr. Sazani was disciplined by the Medical Board of California as described above
and accordingly, Dr. Sazani is in violation of NRS 630.301(3).

9. By reason of the foregoing, Dr. Sazani is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NRS 630.352.

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

1. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners give Dr. Sazani notice of the
charges herein against him and give him notice that he may file an answer to the Complaint herein
as set forth in Section 630.339 of the Nevada Revised Statutes within twenty (20) days of service
of the Complaint.

2. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners set a time and place for a
formal hearing after holding an Early Case Conference pursuant to NRS 630.339(3);

3. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners determine what sanctions it
determines to impose if it determines there has been a violation or violations of the Medical
Practice Act (Nevada Revised Statutes Chapter 630) committed by Dr. Sazani; and

4. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners make, issue and serve on
