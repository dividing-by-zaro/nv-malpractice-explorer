COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, composed
at the time filing was approved of Benjamin J. Rodriguez, M.D., Van V. Heffner and
Beverly Neyland, M.D., having a reasonable basis to believe that Karl Fazekas, M.D., hereinafter
referred to as Dr. Fazekas, has violated the provisions of NRS Chapter 630, hereby issues its
formal Complaint, stating the Investigative Committee's charges and allegations, as follows:

1. Dr. Fazekas currently holds a license to practice medicine in the state of Nevada
(License No. 3298), and has been so licensed since March 13, 1977 by the Nevada State Board of
Medical Examiners pursuant to the provisions of Chapter 630 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

2. Dr. Fazekas resides and practices medicine in the Las Vegas area. His primary
specialty is listed with the Board as internal medicine.

3. Patient Margaret Underwood, a seventy-five year old female, was admitted to
Sunrise Hospital on June 13, 2009 with congestive heart failure. Ms. Underwood has given her
permission to utilize her true identity.

4. Early on the morning of June 14, 2009, Dr. Fazekas entered Ms. Underwood’s
hospital room.

5. Dr. Fazekas spoke to Ms. Underwood regarding her medical history and indicated a
need to examine her. Dr. Fazekas examined Ms. Underwood’s chest area, and while doing so,
fondled her breasts inappropriately. Ms. Underwood told him to stop.

6. Dr. Fazekas lowered his hands on Ms. Underwood’s chest and proceeded to move
his hands down to Ms. Underwood’s pubic region, indicating that she needed a pap smear.
Ms. Underwood told him to stop and attempted to push her hospital call button, however,
Dr. Fazekas moved the button from her reach.

7. Ms. Underwood informed Dr. Fazekas he had no reason to examine her pubic area
and he left her hospital room shortly thereafter.

8. The Las Vegas Metropolitan Police Department arrested Dr. Fazekas on
April 13, 2010, in regard to a criminal complaint filed on February 24, 2010 in Justice Court,
Las Vegas Township. The criminal complaint charges one count of attempted sexual assault, a
felony, one count of coercion, a felony and one count of open or gross lewdness, a gross
misdemeanor, all concerning Ms. Underwood.

9. Patient Kathi Swanson, a fifty-nine year old female, presented to the emergency
room of Sunrise Hospital in the early morning of February 8, 2008 with complaints of nausea for
the past three days and new abdominal pain. Ms. Swanson has given her permission to utilize her
true identity.

10. Ms. Swanson was admitted to Sunrise Hospital for overnight observation after a
CT scan reported negative findings.

11. Dr. Fazekas presented to Ms. Swanson’s hospital room in the late afternoon on
February 8, 2008 to examine her.

12. Dr. Fazekas asked Ms. Swanson about breast examinations and proceeded to
demonstrate how to perform one by touching Ms. Swanson’s breast. Dr. Fazekas then proceeded
to tum Ms. Swanson on her side to perform a rectal examination, instead he examined
Ms. Swanson’s vagina with his fingers and reportedly told her she had a polyp. Dr. Fazekas then
proceeded to perform the rectal examination.

Count I

14. NRS 630.301(5) provides that the engaging by a practitioner in any sexual activity
with a patient who is currently being treated by the practitioner is grounds for the initiation of a
disciplinary action against a licensee.

15. Dr. Fazekas’ inappropriate fondling of Ms. Underwood’s breasts and his touching
of her vaginal area were for no legitimate medical purpose were acts of a sexual nature and
accordingly Dr. Fazekas is in violation of NRS 630.301(5).

16. By reason of the foregoing, Dr. Fazekas is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NRS 630.352.

Count II

17. NRS 630.301(5) provides that the engaging by a practitioner in any sexual activity
with a patient who is currently being treated by the practitioner is grounds for the initiation of a
disciplinary action against a licensee.

18. Dr. Fazekas’ touching of Ms. Swanson’s breast and vaginal area were not for any
legitimate medical purpose and were acts of a sexual nature and accordingly Dr. Fazekas is in
violation of NRS 630.301(5).

19. By reason of the foregoing, Dr. Fazekas is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NRS 630.352.

Count Il

20. NRS 630.301(6) provides that disruptive behavior with patients, if the behavior
interferes with patient care or has an adverse impact on the quality of care rendered to a patient is
grounds for initiating a disciplinary action against a licensee.

21. Dr. Fazekas’ actions towards Ms. Underwood were disruptive and had an adverse
impact on the quality of care rendered to Ms. Underwood and accordingly Dr. Fazekas is in
violation of NRS 630.301(6).
