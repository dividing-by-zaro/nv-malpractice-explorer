COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners (Board),
composed of Charles N. Held, M.D., Theodore B. Berndt, M.D., Valerie J. Clark, by and through
Bradley O. Van Ry, Deputy General Counsel for the Board and attorney for the Investigative
Committee, having a reasonable basis to believe that Abdel Khalek, M.D., hereinafter referred to as
"Respondent," has violated the provisions of NRS Chapter 630, hereby issues its formal Complaint,
stating the Investigative Committee's charges and allegations, as follows:

1, Respondent is in active license status to practice medicine in the state of Nevada,
and has been so licensed since August 14, 1993, and at all times alleged herein, was so licensed
by the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, pursuant to the provisions of Chapter 630 of
the Nevada Revised Statutes.

2. Patient A was a seventy year-old male at the time of the events at issue. His true
identity is not disclosed herein to protect his privacy, but is disclosed in the Patient Designation
served on Respondent along with a copy of this Complaint.

3. Patient B was a sixty-five year old female at the time of the events at issue. Her
true identity is not disclosed herein to protect her privacy, but is disclosed in the Patient
Designation served on Respondent along with a copy of this Complaint.

4. Patient C was a sixty-two year old female at the time of the events at issue. Her
true identity is not disclosed herein to protect her privacy, but is disclosed in the Patient
Designation served on Respondent along with a copy of this Complaint.

5. Patient A presented toRespondent on November 3, 2008 and treated with
Respondent through November, 2008. Respondent failed to maintain timely, legible, accurate
and complete medical records of Patient A.

6. Patient B presented to Respondent on October 24, 2002 and treated with
Respondent through November, 2008. Respondent failed to maintain timely, legible, accurate
and complete medical records of Patient B.

7. Patient C presented to Respondent on June 19, 2000 and treated with Respondent
through August, 2008. Respondent failed to maintain timely, legible, accurate and complete
medical records of Patient C.

COUNT I

8. All of the above allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if
fully set forth herein.

9. Section 630.3062(1) of the Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) provides that failure to
maintain timely, legible, accurate and complete medical records relating to the diagnosis,
treatment and care of a patient, is grounds for initiating disciplinary action.

10. Respondent violated NRS ยง630.3062(1) when he failed to properly maintain
medical records related to Patients A, B and C and is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NRS 630.352.

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

1. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners give Respondent notice of the
charges herein against him and give him notice that he may file an answer to the Complaint
herein as set forth in Section 630.339 of the Nevada Revised Statutes within twenty (20) days of
service of the Complaint.

2. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners set a time and place for a

formal hearing after holding an Early Case Conference pursuant to NRS ยง630.339(3);
