COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Brandee Mooneyhan, J.D., Deputy General Counsel and attorney for the
IC, having a reasonable basis to believe that Christopher Michael Nevarez, M.D., (Respondent)
violated the provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada
Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues
its Complaint, stating the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a medical doctor holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 14036). Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on July 25, 2011.

2. Respondent also holds an active Nevada Controlled Substance Registration,
Certificate No. CS19561, issued by the Nevada State Board of Pharmacy (Pharmacy Board).

3. On June 12, 2019, the Pharmacy Board filed a “Notice of Intended Action and
Accusation” (Pharmacy Board Accusation) alleging that in February 2018, Respondent was the
medical director of Push IV, LLC, in Las Vegas, Nevada, and that in such capacity he violated
various statutes and regulations within the Pharmacy Board’s jurisdiction.


! The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, at the time this formal
Complaint was authorized for filing, was composed of Board members M. Neil Duxbury, Aury Nagy, M.D., and
Michael C. Edwards, M.D.

4. In pertinent part, the Pharmacy Board Accusation alleged that Respondent allowed
registered nurses and paramedics employed by Push IV to access and possess dangerous drugs
without the presence or direct supervision of a licensed practitioner.’

5. The Pharmacy Board Accusation further alleged that with Respondent’s knowledge
and pursuant to his direction, registered nurses and paramedics employed by Push IV administered
dangerous drugs to patients through intravenous therapy and/or injections without Respondent’s
direct supervision and without Respondent or any other practitioner having examined the patients
or having issued written patient-specific and medication-specific orders.

6. Registered nurses are authorized to possess or administer dangerous drugs pursuant
to a practitioner’s order or “pursuant to a chart order, for administration to a patient at another
location.” See NRS 454.213(1)(c).

7. NRS 454.316 renders possession of a dangerous drug unlawful “except that
furnished to the person by a pharmacist pursuant to a legal prescription or by a practitioner.”

8. The Pharmacy Board Accusation asserted that by allowing registered nurses and
paramedics employed by Push IV “to operate Push IV and to use [Respondent’s] authority as a
practitioner to obtain, access, possess and/or store dangerous drugs when he was not on site,
without a proper examination, without direct supervision and before he wrote a patient-specific

17. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

18. Respondent is a practitioner as defined by NRS 639.0125(1), as a physician who
holds a license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada.

19. NRS 630.306(1)(b)(3) provides that engaging in conduct that violates a provision
of Chapter 639 of NRS, or a regulation adopted by the Pharmacy Board pursuant thereto, that is
applicable to a licensee who is a practitioner, as defined in NRS 639.0125, is grounds for initiating
discipline against a licensee.

20.  NRS 639.210(12) prohibits violating, attempting to violate, assisting or abetting in
the violation of, or conspiring to violate any provisions of Chapter 639 of NRS or any law or
regulation relating to drugs, the manufacture or distribution of drugs or the practice of pharmacy.

21. ‘By failing to ensure that dangerous drugs were legally obtained, accessed,
possessed, stored and/or administered to patients of Push IV, Respondent violated laws relating to

22. Pursuant to Chapter 639 of the NRS, the Pharmacy Board adopted NAC 639.945,
wherein certain acts or practices by those holding licenses, certificates, or registrations issued by
