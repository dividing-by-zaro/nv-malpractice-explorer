COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Donald K. White, J.D., Senior Deputy General Counsel and attorney for the
IC, having a reasonable basis to believe that Pamela Elaine Albo, PA-C, (Respondent) violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC)
Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating the IC’s
charges and allegations as follows:

1, Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a physician assistant holding
an active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 608). Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on September 14, 1999.

2. In or about June 2019, the Board became aware that Respondent did not notify the
Board, in writing, within seventy-two (72) hours of the change(s) of her supervising physician(s).

3. Pursuant to NAC 630.360(4), this notification is the responsibility of the physician
assistant.

COUNT I

NRS 630.306(1)(p) — Engaging in Unprofessional Conduct
4, All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.
5, Engaging in any act that is unsafe or unprofessional conduct in accordance with
regulations adopted by the Board constitutes grounds for intitiating disciplinary action pursuant to

NRS 630.306(1)(p).
6. NAC 630.360(4) states:

... a physician assistant shall notify the Board in writing within 72
hours after any change in the supervision of the physician assistant
by a supervising physician.

7. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NRS 630.352.
