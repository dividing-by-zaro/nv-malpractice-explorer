COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee (IC)! of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Ian J. Cumings, J.D., Deputy General Counsel and attorney for the IC,
having a reasonable basis to believe that Anthony Emmanuel Quinn, M.D., (Respondent) violated
the provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and the Nevada Administrative
Code (NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint
(Complaint), stating the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint, a physician licensed to
practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 15814). Respondent was originally
licensed by the Board on April 13, 2015.

A. Respondent’s Treatment of Patient A

2. Patient A was a thirty-two (32) year-old female when she presented to Respondent
for medical care on May 18, 2015. Patient A’s true identity is not disclosed herein to protect her
privacy but is disclosed in the Patient Designation served upon Respondent along with a copy of
this Complaint.


' The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, at the time this formal
Complaint was authorized for filing, was composed of Board members Victor M. Muro, M.D., Chair,
Ms. April Mastroluca, and Weldon Havins, M.D.

3. Patient A presented to Respondent for psychiatric care after reporting symptoms of
depression as a result of an abusive relationship. Patient A was a vulnerable patient with a prior
history of one (1) suicide attempt and a hospitalization for depression.

4, During Patient A’s psychiatric treatment, the Respondent gleaned privileged
information about the patient, including facts pertaining to Patient A’s past including recent and
ongoing dysfunctional relationships. During the course of Patient A’s treatment, Respondent
prescribed sedative hypnotic agents to Patient A.

5. Patient A was last seen by the Respondent on March 17, 2016, for an outpatient
visit, where the Respondent prescribed Patient A Benzodiazepines.

6. Following the March 17, 2016, medication appointment, Respondent began an
inappropriate personal relationship with Patient A, which culminated in the Respondent having
sexual intercourse with Patient A.

COUNT I
NRS 630.301(4) — Malpractice

7. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

8. NRS 630.301(4) provides that malpractice of a Physician is grounds for initiating
disciplinary action against a licensee.

9. NAC 630.040 defines malpractice as “the failure of a physician, in treating a
patient, to use the reasonable care, skill, or knowledge ordinarily used under similar
circumstances.”

10. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent
committed malpractice with respect to the treatment of Patient A by engaging in an inappropriate
personal and sexual relationship with a vulnerable patient who had recently been under the
psychiatric care of the Respondent.

11. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT If
NRS 630.301(7) — Violation of Patient Trust and Exploitation of Physician and Patient
Relationship for Financial or Personal Gain

12. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

13. | NRS 630.301(7) provides that “engaging in conduct that violates the trust of a
patient and exploits the relationship between the physician and the patient for financial or other
personal gain” is grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

14. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent
violated the trust of a patient and exploited the relationship between the Respondent and Patient A
by gaining intimate knowledge of Patient A during psychiatric treatment, and subsequently
engaging in a sexual relationship with her.

15. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.
