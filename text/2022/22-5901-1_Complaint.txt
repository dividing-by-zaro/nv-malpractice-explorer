The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners

COMPLAINT

(Board), by and through Aaron Bart Fricke, J.D., General Counsel and attorney for the IC, having a
reasonable basis to believe that Robert Stephen Fredericks, M.D., (Respondent) violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC)
Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating the IC’s
charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a medical doctor holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 4014). Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on March 29, 1980.

2. NRS 639.23507 provides in pertinent part that a physician shall, before issuing an
initial prescription for a controlled substance listed in schedule II, III or IV or an opioid that is a
controlled substance listed in schedule V and at least once every 90 days thereafter for the
duration of the course of treatment using the controlled substance, obtain a patient utilization
report regarding the patient from the computerized program established by the Nevada State
Board of Pharmacy and the Investigation Division of the Department of Public Safety pursuant to

NRS 453.162 (“Nevada Prescription Monitoring Program”).

' The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, at the time this formal
Complaint was authorized for filing, was composed of Board members Victor M. Muro, M.D., Rachakonda Prabhu,
M.D., and Ms. Sandy Peltyn.

3. Pursuant to NRS 639.23507(1)(a)-(b), a practitioner shall:

(a) Review the patient utilization report; and

(b) Determine whether the patient has been issued another
prescription for the same controlled substance that provides for
ongoing treatment using the controlled substance. If the
practitioner determines from the patient utilization report or from
any other source that the patient has been issued such a
prescription, the practitioner shall not prescribe the controlled
substance unless the practitioner determines that issuing the
prescription is medically necessary.

4, Patient A? was a male in his late sixties and early seventies at the time of the events
at issue.

5. During the period of July 31, 2018, through May 21, 2021, a period in excess of
thirty-three (33) months, Respondent did not obtain a patient utilization report regarding Patient A
from the Nevada Prescription Monitoring Program.

6. During this period, Respondent did not review a patient utilization report for
Patient A from the Nevada Prescription Monitoring Program, and did not determine whether
Patient A had been issued another prescription for the same controlled substances that provides for
ongoing treatment using the controlled substance.

7. During this period, Respondent did not once consider the readily available
information provided by the Nevada Prescription Monitoring Program regarding the totality of
controlled substances Patient A was being prescribed by himself and others.

8. During this period, Respondent issued eighty-five (85) prescriptions for controlled
substances scheduled II through IV to Patient A, on an approximately monthly basis, consisting
variously of Clonazepam (schedule IV), Belsomra (IV), Testosterone (III), Lorazepam (IV), and
Zolpidem (IV).

10. | NRS 630.3062(1)(h) provides that fraudulent, illegal, unauthorized or otherwise
inappropriate prescribing, administering or dispensing of a controlled substance listed in schedule
II, III or IV constitutes grounds for initiating disciplinary action or denying licensure.

11. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, during the period
of July 31, 2018, through May 21, 2021, Respondent issued to Patient A eighty-five (85)
prescriptions for controlled substances listed in schedules II through IV.

12. Respondent’s prescribing to Patient A during this period, without accessing the
Nevada Prescription Monitoring Program, without obtaining and personally reviewing Patient A’s
patient utilization report, and perforce without once considering, for more than thirty-three (33)
months, the readily available information provided by the Nevada Prescription Monitoring
Program regarding the totality of controlled substances Patient A was being prescribed by himself
and others during that period, among other deficiencies, was unauthorized and inappropriate.

13. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT II
NRS 630.3062(1)(g) - Failure to Comply With the Requirements of NRS 639.23507

14. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

15. | NRS 630.3062(1)(g) provides that failure to comply with the requirements of
NRS 639.23507, among others, constitutes initiating disciplinary action or denying licensure.

16. With respect to his care and treatment of Patient A, Respondent failed to comply
with the requirements of NRS 639.23507, specifically, NRS 639.23507(1)(a)-(b).

17. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.
