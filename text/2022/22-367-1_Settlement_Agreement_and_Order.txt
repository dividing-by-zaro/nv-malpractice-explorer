SETTLEMENT AGREEMENT

The Investigative Committee (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Donald K. White, J.D., Senior Deputy General Counsel for the Board and
attorney for the IC, and Pamela Elaine Albo, PA-C (Respondent), a licensed physician assistant in
Nevada, assisted by her attorney, Bridget Ann Kelly, Esq., of the law firm of Nutile Law, hereby
enter into this Settlement Agreement (Agreement) based on the following:!

A. BACKGROUND

1, Respondent is a physician assistant currently licensed in active status by the Board
pursuant to Chapter 630 of the Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) and Chapter 630 of the Nevada
Administrative Code (NAC) (collectively, the Medical Practice Act) to practice medicine in
Nevada. Her license was originally issued on September 14, 1999, (License No. 608).

2. On March 1, 2022, in Case No. 22-367-1, the IC filed a formal Complaint
(Complaint) charging Respondent with violating the Medical Practice Act. The Complaint alleges
one (1) violation of NRS 630.306(1)(p), Engaging in Unprofessional Conduct, specifically,
NAC 630.360(4) requires a physician assistant to notify the Board in writing within seventy-two

3. Respondent was properly served with a copy of this Complaint, has reviewed and

B. TERMS & CONDITIONS

NOW, THEREFORE, in order to resolve the matters addressed herein, i.e., the matters
with regard to the Complaint, Respondent and the IC hereby agree to the following terms and
conditions:

1. Jurisdiction. Respondent is, and at all times relevant to the Complaint has been, a
physician licensed to practice medicine in Nevada subject to the jurisdiction of the Board as set
forth in the Medical Practice Act.

2. Representation _by _Counsel/Knowing, Willing and_Intelligent_Agreement.
Respondent acknowledges she is represented by counsel and wishes to resolve the matters
addressed herein with said counsel. Respondent agrees that if representation by counsel in this
matter materially changes prior to entering into this Agreement and for the duration of this
Agreement, that counsel for the IC will be timely notified of the material change. Respondent
agrees that she knowingly, willingly and intelligently enters into this Agreement after deciding to
have a full consultation with and upon the advice of legal counsel.

3. Waiver of Rights. In connection with this Agreement, and the associated terms
and conditions, Respondent knowingly, willingly and intelligently waives all rights in connection
with this administrative matter. Respondent hereby knowingly, willingly and intelligently waives
all rights arising under the United States Constitution, the Nevada Constitution, the Medical
Practice Act, the OML, the APA, and any other legal rights that may be available to her or that
may apply to her in connection with the administrative proceedings resulting from the Complaint
filed in this matter, including defense of the Complaint, adjudication of the allegations set forth in
the Complaint, and imposition of any disciplinary actions or sanctions ordered by the Board.
Respondent agrees to settle and resolve the allegations of the Complaint as set out by this
Agreement, without a hearing or any further proceedings and without the right to judicial review.

4. Acknowledgement of Reasonable Basis to Proceed. As of the time of entering

into this Settlement Agreement, the allegations of the Complaint remain unproven. Respondent

a. For the purposes of this Settlement Agreement, Respondent admits that

b. Respondent will pay the costs and expenses incurred in the investigation
and prosecution of the above-referenced matter within sixty (60) days of the Board’s acceptance,
adoption and approval of this Agreement, the current amount being one thousand two hundred
seven dollars and thirty-eight cents ($1,207.38).

c. Respondent shall pay a fine of five hundred dollars ($500.00) within sixty
(60) days of the Board’s acceptance, adoption and approval of this Agreement.

d. The Respondent shall perform four (4) hours of Continued Medical
Education (CME), related to the subject of proper recordkeeping, in addition to the required
CMEs for licensure. These CME hours must be completed within six (6) months of the Board’s
acceptance, adoption and approval of this Agreement.

e. This Agreement shall be reported to the appropriate entities and parties as

required by law, including, but not limited to, the National Practitioner Data Bank.
