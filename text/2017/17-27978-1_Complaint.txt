COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners

(Board), hereby issues this formal Complaint against Ali Kia, M.D. (hereinafter referred to as

Dr. Kia or Respondent), a licensed physician in Nevada. After investigating this matter, the IC

has a reasonable basis to believe that Respondent has violated provisions of Nevada Revised

Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively,

the Medical Practice Act).

The IC alleges the following facts:

1, Respondent is a medical doctor currently licensed in active status (License No.

11940). Respondent was issued his license from the Board on July 7, 2006, pursuant to NRS

Chapter 630.
2. Respondent submitted his California application for licensure on March 3, 2016.
3. Effective October 10, 2016, the Medical Board of California issued to Respondent

a Decision and Order, accepting a Stipulation for a Probationary License, based on Respondent’s
failure to disclose required information regarding his criminal history and medical education on
his Physician’s and Surgeon’s application on March 03, 2016.

Count I
NRS 630.301(3)
(Disciplinary Action By Another Licensing Board)

4. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

5. NRS 630.301(3) provides that any disciplinary action, including, without
limitation, the revocation, suspension, modification or limitation of a license to practice any type
of medicine, taken by another state ... by any licensing authority is grounds for initiating
disciplinary action or denying licensure.

6. Respondent was disciplined by the Medical Board of California for failure to
disclose required information regarding his criminal history and medical education on his
Physician’s and Surgeon’s application on March 3, 2016.

7. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NRS 630.352.

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

1. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners give Respondent notice of the
charges herein against him and give him notice that he may file an answer to the Complaint herein
as set forth in NRS 630.339(2) within twenty (20) days of service of the Complaint;

2. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners set a time and place for a
formal hearing after holding an Early Case Conference pursuant to NRS 630.339(3);

3. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners determine what sanctions to
impose if it determines there has been a violation or violations of the Medical Practice Act
committed by Respondent;

4. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners make, issue and serve on
Respondent its findings of fact, conclusions of law and order, in writing, that includes the
sanctions imposed; and
