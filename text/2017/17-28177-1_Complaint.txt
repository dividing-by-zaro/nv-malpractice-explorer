COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners,
having a reasonable basis to believe that Jose Hiram Alvarez, M.D. (hereinafter referred to as
Dr. Alvarez or Respondent), has violated the provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS)
Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical
Practice Act), hereby issues its formal Complaint, stating the IC’s charges and allegations, as
follows:

Respondent’s Licensure Status

1. Respondent is currently licensed in active status (License No. 10765), and was so
licensed on December 5, 2003, by the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, pursuant to the
provisions of NRS Chapter 630.

2. On or about January 22, 2016, the Arizona Medical Board issued Findings of Fact,
Conclusions of Law and Order for Letter of Reprimand in the Matter of Jose H. Alvarez, M.D.
(Case No. MD-14-1074A) for failing to maintain adequate medical records on a patient.

3. At no time did Respondent report in writing to the Nevada State Board of Medical

Examiners that disciplinary action had been taken against him by the Arizona Medical Board.

' The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, at the time the filing of this
Complaint was approved, was composed of Wayne Hardwick, M.D., Theodore B. Berndt, M.D., and Mr. M. Neil
Duxbury.

Count I

4, All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

5. NRS 630.301(3) provides that any disciplinary action, including the revocation,
suspension, modification or limitation of a license to practice any type of medicine, taken by
another state, is grounds for initiating disciplinary action against a licensee.

6. The Arizona Medical Board issued Findings of Fact, Conclusions of Law and Order
for Letter of Reprimand on or about January 22, 2016, regarding Respondent’s failure to maintain
adequate records on a patient which is disciplinary action taken by another state.

7. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NRS 630.352.

Count If

8. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

9. NRS 630.306(1)(k) provides that failure by a licensee or applicant to report in
writing, within 30 days, any disciplinary action taken against the licensee or applicant by another
state, the Federal Government or a foreign country, including, without limitation, the revocation,
suspension or surrender of a license to practice medicine in another jurisdiction, is grounds for
initiating disciplinary action.

10. Respondent was disciplined by the Arizona Medical Board as described above.

11. Respondent failed to timely report the disciplinary action taken by the Arizona
Medical Board.

12. Accordingly, Respondent is in violation of NRS 630.306(1)(k).

13. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NRS 630.352.
