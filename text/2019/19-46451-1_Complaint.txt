COMPLAINT

‘The Investigative Committee (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners (Board)
hereby issues this formal Complaint (Complaint) against Enrique Feria-Arias, M.D. (Respondent), a
licensed physictan in Nevada. After investigating this matter, the IC! has a reasonable basis to
believe that Respondent has violated provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and
Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively Medical Practice Act). The IC
alleges the following facts:

1. Respondent was licensed by the Board, pursuant to the provisions of the Medical
Practice Act, on January 10, 2017, and is currently licensed in active status (License No. 16883).

2. On August 24, 2018, Respondent voluntarily surrendered his California Medical

Board license due to his inability to satisfy the terms and conditions of the California probationary
license issued to him on September 28, 2017.

COUNTI
NRS 630.301(3) (Out of State Discipline Imposed)

3. Allof the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

4. NRS 630.301(3) provides that disciplinary action taken by other jurisdiction
constitutes grounds for initiating disciplinary action.

5. NRS 630.301(3) specifically provides any disciplinary action, including, without
limitation, the revocation, suspension, modification or limitation of a license to practice any type
of medicine, taken by another state, the Federal Government, a foreign country or any other
jurisdiction or the surrender of the license or discontinuing the practice of medicine while under
investigation by any licensing authority, a medical facility, a branch of the Armed Services of the
United States, an insurance company, an agency of the Federal Government or any employer.

6. Respondent was disciplined by the California Medical Board on August 24, 2018,

which constitutes grounds for initiating disciplinary action against a licensee.

7. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent 1s subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.
COUNT IT

NRS 630.306(1)(k) (Failure to Report Out-of-State Discipline Imposed)

8 All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by’
reference as though fully set forth herein.

9. NRS 630.306(1)(k) provides that failure by a licensee to report in writing, within
30 days, any disciplinary action taken against the licensee by another state constitutes grounds for
initiating disciplinary action.

10. Respondent was disciplined by the California Medical Board on August 24, 2018,
and was required to inform the Board of the aforementioned discipline in accordance with NRS
630.306(1)(k).

11. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as

provided in NRS 630.352.
