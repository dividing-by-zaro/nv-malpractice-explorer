COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee’ (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Aaron Bart Fricke, Esq., Deputy General Counsel and attorney for the IC,
having a reasonable basis to believe that Hans-Jorg Walter Rosler, M.D. (Respondent) violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC)
Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating the IC’s
charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relevant to this Complaint, a physician licensed to
practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 10364). His license was originally issued
by the Board on November 26, 2002.

A. Respondent’s Failure to Supervise a Physician Assistant

2. At all times relevant to this Complaint, Respondent supervised Steven Lee
Olenchak, PA-C, a physician assistant licensed by the Board (Olenchak), pursuant to a formal
notice of supervision provided to the Board in accord with NAC 630.360(4), wherein Respondent
certified that he had read and was aware of all provisions of NRS Chapter 630 and

NAC Chapter 630 concerning his duties as a physician supervising Olenchak.

Aury Nagy, M.D., and Michael C. Edwards, M.D., FACS.

3. Pursaunt to NAC 630.370(5), Respondent was required to develop and carry out a
program to ensure the quality of care provided by Olenchak, and to maintain accurate records and
documentation regarding his program of supervision of Olenchak.

4. Respondent failed to develop and carry out a program to ensure the quality of care
provided by Olenchak, and to maintain accurate records and documentation regarding his program
of supervision of Olenchak.

COUNT I
NRS 630.3065(2)(a) (Knowing Failure to Comply With A Regulation of the Board)

5. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

6. Pursuant to NRS 630.3065(2)(a), knowingly failing to comply with a regulation of
the Board is grounds for disciplinary action against a licensee.

7. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent
knowingly failed to comply with NAC 630.370(5).

8. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

B. Respondent’s Responsibility for Improper Care by Supervisee Physician Assistant

9. Patient A’s true identity is not disclosed herein to protect his or her privacy, but is
disclosed in the Patient Designation served upon Respondent along with a copy of this Complaint.

10. Patient A was a close personal friend of Olenchak, who also treated Patient A for
chronic pain with opioid analgesics, along with amphetamines and other controlled substances.

11. Patient A suffered from opioid dependence, polysubstance abuse disorder,
substance-induced mood disorder, and depressive disorder, among other ailments.

12. On July 27, 2014, Patient A died in a motor vehicle collision from multiple blunt
force injuries to the head. At the time of the crash, Patient A was under the influence of the
controlled substances that Olenchak prescribed.

13. Pursuant to the Board’s Order of September 6, 2019, in Case No. 19-22430-1,

Olenchak admitted, and the Board formally found, that Olenchak violated standards of practice

regarding the prescription of opioid analgesics to Patient A (see NRS 630.306(1)(b)(2)), and failed
to maintain proper records for Patient A (see NRS 630.3062(1)(a)), for which conduct Olenchak
was formally disciplined by the Board.

14. Pursuant to NAC 630.370, as Olenchak’s supervising physician, Respondent is
responsible for all the medical activities of Olenchak, including his care of Patient A and
maintainence of medical records for Patient A.

15. Pursuant to NAC 630.375, Olenchak is considered to be and is deemed the agent
of Respondent in the performance of all medical activities, including his care of Patient A and
maintainence of medical records for Patient A.

COUNT II
NRS 630.306(1)(b)(2) (Violation of Standards of Practice)

16. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by reference
as though fully set forth herein.

17. A physician assistant is subject to discipline for violating a provision of NRS
630.301 through 630.3065 inclusive, pursuant to NAC 630.380(1)(m).

18. Violation of a standard of practice adopted by the Board is grounds for disciplinary
action pursuant to NRS 630.306(1)(b)(2).

19. | The Board adopted by reference the Model Policy on the Use of Opioid Analgesics
in the Treatment of Chronic Pain, July 2013, published by the Federation of State Medical Boards
of the United States, Inc. (Model Policy).

20. Pursuant to NAC 630.230(1)(k), a licensee shall not engage in the practice of
writing prescriptions for controlled substances to treat chronic pain in a manner that deviates from
the policies set forth in the Model Policy on the Use of Opioid Analgesics in the Treatment of
Chronic Pain adopted by reference in NAC 630.187.

21. Olenchak wrote prescriptions to Patient A for opioid analgesics to treat chronic
pain in a manner that deviated from the Model Policy.

22. + Pursuant to NAC 630.370 and 630.375, Respondent is professionally responsible
for his agent Olenchak writing prescriptions to Patient A for opioid analgesics to treat chronic pain
in a manner that deviated from the Model Policy.

23. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT HI
NRS 630.3062(1)(a) (Failure to Maintain Proper Medical Records)

24, All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

25. NRS 630.3062(1)(a) provides that the failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate
and complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient is grounds
for initiating disciplinary action against a licensee.

26.  Olenchak failed to maintain complete medical records relating to the diagnosis,
treatment and care of Patient A, by failing to document his actions in complying with the Model
Policy, including, but not limited to, failing to document the following: physical examinations
before prescribing opioid analgesics; queries of the PMP before prescribing opioid analgesics;
urinalysis before and after prescribing opioid analgesics; support for his diagnoses with physical
examination findings; treatment objectives to evaluate treatment progress; monitoring and
adapting his treatment plan; progress toward discontinuation of opioid therapy.

27. Pursuant to NAC 630.370 and 630.375, Respondent is professionally responsible
for his agent Olenchak’s failure to maintain complete medical records relating to the diagnosis,
treatment and care of Patient A.

28. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.
