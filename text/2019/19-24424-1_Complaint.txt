COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Aaron Bart Fricke, Esq., Deputy General Counsel and attorney for the IC,
having a reasonable basis to believe that Lane Fielding Smith, M.D. (Respondent) violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC)
Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its complaint, stating the IC’s
charges and allegations as follows:

l. Respondent was at all times relevant to this Complaint, a physician licensed to
practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 10065). His license was originally issued
by the Board on January 8, 2002.

A. Respondent’s Treatment of Patient A

2. Patient A was a close personal friend of Respondent, and a patient of Respondent.
Respondent ended his physician-patient relationship with Patient A on June 28, 2014. Patient A’s
true identity is not disclosed herein to protect his or her privacy, but is disclosed in the Patient
Designation served upon Respondent along with a copy of this Complaint.

3. Respondent prescribed Lexapro, an anti-depressant, to Patient A on or about
November 25, 2014, when Respondent no longer had a bona fide physician-patient relationship
with Patient A.

4, Respondent failed to maintain medical records relating to the diagnosis and
treatment of Patient A for the November 25, 2014 prescription.

5. Respondent prescribed Lexapro, with 5 refills, and Valium, a schedule IV
controlled substance, to Patient A on or about February 15, 2015, when Respondent no longer had
a bona fide physician-patient relationship with Patient A.

6. Respondent failed to maintain medical records relating to the diagnosis and
treatment of Patient A for the February 15, 2015 prescription.

COUNT I
NRS 630.3062(1)(a) (Failure to Maintain Proper Medical Records)

7. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by reference
as if fully set forth herein.

8. NRS 630.3062(1)(a) provides that the failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate
and complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient is grounds
for initiating discipline against a licensee.

9. Respondent failed to maintain complete medical records relating to the diagnosis,
treatment and care of Patient A for his prescription of Lexapro on November 25, 2014, and his
prescriptions of Lexapro and Valium on or about February 15, 2015.

10. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

1. That the Board give Respondent notice of the charges herein and give notice that
an answer to the Complaint herein may be filed as set forth in NRS 630.339(2) within twenty (20)
days of service of the Complaint;

2. That the Board set a time and place for a formal hearing after holding an Early

Case Conference pursuant to NRS 630.339(3);
