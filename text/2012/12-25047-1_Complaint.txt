COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), composed, at the time of filing, of Theodore B. Berndt, M.D., Chairman,
Valerie J. Clark, BSN, RHU, LUTCF, Member, and Michael J. Fischer, M.D., Member, by and
through Erin L. Albright, Esq., Deputy General Counsel and Attorney for the IC, having a
reasonable basis to believe that Kevin J. Thorn, P.A.-C (Respondent), violated the provisions of
Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 (Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its formal
Complaint, stating the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

FACTUAL BACKGROUND

The following factual background is applicable to a determination in this matter.

A. Respondent’s Licensure Status

1. Respondent is currently licensed in active status (License No. PA717), and has
been so licensed by the Board since February 13, 2002, pursuant to the provisions of the Medical
Practice Act.

B. Respondent’s First Arrest for Driving and/or Being in Actual Physical Control
of a Vehicle While Under the Influence of a Controlled Substance

2. On August 5, 2009, Respondent was arrested for driving and/or being in actual
physical control of a vehicle while under the influence of a controlled substance (DUI Drugs), a
violation of NRS 484C.110, a misdemeanor. (See a true and correct copy of the Criminal
Complaint attached hereto as Exhibit 1.) Respondent failed to report this arrest to the Board.

3. On October 29, 2009, a Criminal Complaint charging Respondent with one count
of DUI Drugs was filed in the Justice Court of the Las Vegas Township as Case No. 09m32887X.
(See a true and correct copy of the Docket attached hereto as Exhibit 2.) Respondent failed to
report this Criminal Complaint to the Board.

4. On February 8, 2010, the court found Respondent guilty of DUI Drugs. Id.
Respondent failed to report this conviction to the Board.

Cc. Respondent’s Arrest for Child Abuse/Neglect

5. On May 8, 2010, Respondent was arrested on two counts of child abuse/neglect,
both of which are a violation of NRS 200.508, a gross misdemeanor. (See a true and correct copy
of the Complaint attached hereto as Exhibit 3.) Respondent failed to report this arrest to the
Board.

6. On May 11, 2010, a Criminal Complaint charging Respondent with two counts of
Child Endangerment, both of which are a violation of NRS 200.508, a gross misdemeanor, was
filed in the Justice Court of Las Vegas Township as Case No. 10F08869X. Id. Respondent failed
to report this Complaint to the Board.

7. On June 23, 2010, the State amended count I of the Complaint to misdemeanor
Disorderly Conduct and dismissed count II of the Complaint. (See a true and correct copy of the
Docket attached hereto as Exhibit 4.) The court found Respondent guilty of misdemeanor
Disorderly Conduct. Id. Respondent failed to report this conviction to the Board.

COUNT I
(Two Counts)

17. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

18. | Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) 630.380(1)(m) provides that a violation of
NRS 630.301 to 630.3065, inclusive, is grounds for initiating disciplinary action against a
physician assistant licensee.

19. | NRS 630.304(1) provides that obtaining, maintaining or renewing or attempting to
obtain, maintain or renew a license to practice medicine by bribery, fraud or misrepresentation or
by any false, misleading, inaccurate or incomplete statement is grounds for initiating discipline
against a licensee.

20. | Respondent was arrested on August 5, 2009 and charged with DUI Drugs, a
violation of NRS 484.379, a misdemeanor.

21. On February 8, 2010, Respondent was convicted of misdemeanor DUI Drugs.

22. Respondent was arrested on May 8, 2010 and charged with two counts of child

abuse/neglect, two violations of NRS 200.508, a gross misdemeanor.

23. On June 23, 2010, the State amended the first count of child abuse/neglect to
disorderly conduct and dismissed the second count of child abuse/neglect. Respondent was
convicted of one count of disorderly conduct, a misdemeanor.

24. On his application to renew his physician assistant license, Respondent provided
false, misleading and/or inaccurate information when he answered the question regarding whether
he had been arrested, investigated for, charged with, convicted of, or pled guilty or nolo
contendere to any state or local law that is a misdemeanor, gross misdemeanor or felony in the
negative.

25. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NAC 630.410.

COUNT II
(Six Counts)

26. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

27. NAC 630.380(1)(m) provides that a violation of NRS 630.301 to 630.3065,
inclusive, is grounds for initiating disciplinary action against a physician assistant licensee.

28. NRS 630.306(12) provides that failure to report to the Board in writing, within
thirty (30) days, any criminal action taken or conviction obtained against the licensee, other than a
minor traffic violation, in this State is grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

29. Respondent failed to report to the Board in writing, within thirty (30) days, the
following criminal actions taken against him: a) August 5, 2009 arrest and subsequent conviction
for DUI Drugs; b) May 8, 2010 arrest for two counts of child abuse/neglect and subsequent
conviction for one count of disorderly conduct; c) September 3, 2011 arrest and pending Criminal
Complaint for DUI Drugs; d) December 23, 2011 arrest and pending Criminal Complaint for DUI
Drugs; e) January 25, 2012 arrest and pending Criminal Complaint for DUI Drugs; and f) June 9,
2012 arrest and pending Criminal Complaint for DUI Drugs.

30. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NAC 630.410.

36. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

37. NAC 630.380(1)(m) provides that a violation of NRS 630.301 to 630.3065,
inclusive, is grounds for initiating disciplinary action against a physician assistant licensee.

38. NRS 630.306(2)(a) provides that engaging in any conduct which is intended to
deceive is grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

39.  Respondent’s failure to disclose to the Board his criminal arrests for two (2) counts
of child abuse/neglect and five (5) drug-related DUIs and the formal charges against him
constitute conduct that is intended to deceive.

40. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NAC 630.410.

42. The Board has the authority to adopt regulations regarding the grounds and
procedures respecting disciplinary actions against physician assistants. NRS 630.275(7).

43. NAC 630.380(1)(i) provides that being convicted of a violation of any state law
regulating the use of a controlled substance is grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

44. On February 8, 2010, Respondent was convicted of violating a Nevada law that
prohibits driving while under the influence of a controlled substance.

2. That the Board set a time and place for a formal hearing after holding an
Early Case Conference pursuant to NRS 630.339(3);

3. That the Board determine the sanctions it will impose if it finds Respondent

4. That the Board make, issue and serve on Respondent, in writing, its findings of
