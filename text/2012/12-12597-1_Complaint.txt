COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners ("Board"),
composed at the time of filing of Theodore B. Bermdt, M.D., Chairman,
Valerie J. Clark, BSN, RHU, LUTCF, Member, and Michael J. Fischer, M.D., Member, by and
through Bradley O. Van Ry, Deputy General Counsel, having a reasonable basis to believe that
Maria Corazon Orozco Regalado, M.D., hereinafter referred to as “Respondent,” has violated the
provisions of NRS Chapter 630, hereby issues its formal Complaint, stating the Investigative
Committee's charges and allegations, as follows:

1. Respondent, is currently licensed in active status (License No. 8966), and has been
so licensed since April 27, 1999 by the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners pursuant to the
provisions of Chapter 630 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

2. On April 9, 2011, a person in North Las Vegas, Nevada died as a result of cosmetic
surgery performed by two persons not licensed to practice medicine in the state of Nevada. The
persons performing the surgery were Ruben Mattallana-Galvas and Carmen Torres-Sanchez. Both
Mr. Mattallana-Galvas and Ms. Torres-Sanchez plead guilty to involuntary manslaughter on
August 4, 2011 as a result of their actions causing the death of the patient.

3. Respondent had, on at least one occasion, received medical treatment from

Mr. Mattallana-Galvas. Additionally, Respondent had a previous filial, personal and/or separate

4. Unfortunately, Respondent never reported the unlicensed medical practices of both
of the foregoing to the medical board or any criminal authority at anytime.

Count I

5. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

6. Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.305(1)(e) provides that aiding, assisting,
employing or advising, directly or indirectly, any unlicensed person to engage in the practice of
medicine contrary to the provisions of this chapter or the regulations of the Board is grounds for
initiating discipline against a licensee.

7. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in Section 630.352 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

Count II

8. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

9. Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.3065(3) provides that the willful failure to
perform a statutory or other legal obligation imposed upon a licensed physician is grounds for
initiating discipline against a licensee.

10. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in Section 630.352 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

Count Il

11. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

12. Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.3062(6) provides that the failure to report any
person the licensee knows, or has reason to know, is in violation of the provisions of this chapter

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

1. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners give Respondent notice of the
charges herein against her and give her notice that she may file an answer to the Complaint herein
as set forth in Section 630.339 of the Nevada Revised Statutes within twenty (20) days of service
of the Complaint.

2. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners set a time and place for a
formal hearing after holding an Early Case Conference pursuant to NRS §630.339(3);

3. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners determine what sanctions it
determines to impose if it determines there has been a violation or violations of the
Medical Practice Act (Nevada Revised Statutes Chapter 630) committed by Respondent; and

4. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners make, issue and serve on
Respondent its findings of facts, conclusions of law and order, in writing, that includes the
sanctions imposed; and

5. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners take such other and further
action as may be just and proper in these premises.
