COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee’ (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners (Board)
hereby issues this formal Complaint (Complaint) against Maria Corazon Orozco Regalado, M.D.
(Respondent), a licensed physician in Nevada. After investigating this matter, the IC has a
reasonable basis to believe that Respondent has violated provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes
(NRS) chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice
Act). The IC alleges the following facts:

1. Respondent is currently licensed in active status (License No. 8966), and has been
licensed by the Board since April 27, 1999, pursuant to the provisions of the Medical Practice Act.

2. In a letter to the Board dated August 11, 2014, Respondent admitted having left
pre-signed prescription forms for her advanced practice registered nurses (APRNs), to use while
Respondent was on emergency family leave in the Philippines from April 28, 2014, through May
23, 2014.

3. The Board may initiate disciplinary action when it finds a licensee signed a blank
prescription form. NRS 630.304(4)

4. Based on the foregoing factual allegations, Respondent violated NRS 630.304(4)
when she signed blank prescription forms to be used by her APRNs while she was in the
Philippines on emergency family leave.

5. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

1. That the Board give Respondent notice of the charges herein against her and give
her notice that she may file an answer to the Complaint herein as set forth in NRS 630.339(2)
within 20 days of service of the Complaint;

2. That the Board set a time and place for a formal hearing after holding an
Early Case Conference pursuant to NRS 630.339(3);

3. That the Board determine the sanctions it will impose if it finds Respondent
violated the Medical Practice Act;

4. That the Board make, issue and serve on Respondent, in writing, its findings of
fact, conclusions of law and order, which shall include the sanctions imposed; and

5. That the Board take such other and further action as may be just and proper in these

premises.
