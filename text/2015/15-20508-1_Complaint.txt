COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), composed at the time of filing of Theodore B. Berndt, M.D., Valerie J. Clark, BSN,
RHU, LUTCF, and Michael J. Fischer, M.D., by and through Erin L. Albright, Esq., General
Counsel and attorney for the IC, having a reasonable basis to believe that Ara Keshishian, M.D.
(Respondent), violated the provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada
Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues
its Complaint, stating the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent is currently licensed in active status (License No. 9900), and has been
so licensed by the Board since July 20, 2001, pursuant to the provisions of the Medical Practice
Act.

2. At the times alleged herein, Respondent was also licensed to practice medicine in
the state of California.

3. On or about July 11, 2014, Respondent and the Medical Board of California
entered into a Stipulated Settlement and Disciplinary Order (Settlement) whereby Respondent’s
medical license was revoked for a period of three (3) years, with said revocation stayed pending
the following terms and conditions: Respondent shall complete not less than forty (40) hours per

year, for each year of probation, a Board preapproved Category I certified education

4, If Respondent fails to fully comply with any term or condition of probation, the
Medical Board of California may rescind probation and revoke Respondent’s California medical
license.

5. On or about July 11, 2014, the Settlement was adopted as the Decision and Order
of the Medical Board of California.

6. To date, Respondent has not violated any terms or conditions of the Settlement.

7. Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.301(3) provides that any disciplinary action,
including, without limitation, the revocation, suspension, modification or limitation of a license to
practice any type of medicine, taken by another state is grounds for initiating discipline against a
licensee.

8. The Settlement constitutes disciplinary action taken by another state that is grounds
for initiating discipline against a licensee.

9. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.
