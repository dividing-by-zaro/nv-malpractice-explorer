COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners (Board)
hereby issues this formal Complaint (Complaint) against Heather L. Rohrer (Respondent), a licensed
physician assistant in Nevada. After investigating this matter, the IC has a reasonable basis to
believe that Respondent has violated provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) chapter 630 and
Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) chapter 630 (collectively Medical Practice Act). The IC
alleges the following facts:

1. Respondent was licensed by the Board on August 1, 2003 (License No. PA789),
pursuant to the provisions of the Medical Practice Act, and is currently licensed in active status.

2. Patient A was a 10-year-old male at the time the incidents in question commenced.
His true identity is not disclosed in this Complaint to protect his identity, but his identity is
disclosed in the Patient Designation contemporaneously served on Respondent with this
Complaint.

3. Patient A is Respondent’s stepson.

4, From January 13, 2011 through August 2011, Respondent prescribed methylphenidate,

brand name Concentra, a schedule II controlled substance, to Patient A.

' The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners at the time of authorizing this
Complaint was composed of Board members Theodore B. Berndt, M.D., Chairman, Valerie J. Clark, BSN, RHU,
LUTCF, Member, and Michael J. Fischer, M.D., Member.

5. From April 30, 2012 through August 2012, Respondent prescribed methylphenidate,
brand name Concentra, a schedule II controlled substance, to Patient A.

6. Respondent failed to keep medical records for her care and treatment of Patient A.

7. Based on the foregoing, the IC charges Respondent with the following violations of

the Medical Practice Act:

Count I
8. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.
9. The Board may initiate disciplinary action when a licensee administers, dispenses

or prescribes a controlled substance to herself or others except as authorized by law.
NRS 630.306(3).

10. A physician assistant shall not prescribe schedule II controlled substances to her
child except in cases of emergency. NRS 453.381(1).

11. Respondent prescribed a schedule II controlled substance to her stepson for eight
consecutive months in 2011 and for five consecutive months in 2012, in violation of NRS 453.381(1).

12. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NAC 630.410.

Count II

13. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

14. | NRS 630.3062(1) provides that the failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate and
complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient is grounds for
initiating discipline against a licensee.

15. Respondent failed to maintain timely, legible, accurate and complete medical
records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of Patient A as outlined above.

16. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NAC 630.410.
