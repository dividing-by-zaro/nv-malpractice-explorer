COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee’ (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board) hereby issues this formal Complaint (Complaint) against Rogelio Machuca, M.D.
(Respondent), a licensed physician in Nevada. After investigating this matter, the IC has a
reasonable basis to believe that Respondent has violated provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes
(NRS) chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) chapter 630 (collectively Medical
Practice Act). The IC alleges the following facts:

1. Respondent was licensed by the Board, pursuant to the provisions of the Medical
Practice Act, on July 1, 2011, and is currently licensed in active status (License No. 13983).

2. At all times alleged herein, Respondent was working as a physician at Silver Sky
Assisted Living Center in Las Vegas, Nevada.

3. On or about July 10, 2014, staff at Silver Sky Assisted Living found nine signed
prescription forms that were lacking patient identification or orders. These prescription forms were
titled “Machuca Family Medicine-Hospice & Pallative Care.”

4. The signature on each signed prescription form belonged to Respondent.

5. Based on the foregoing, the IC charges Respondent with the following violation of

6. NRS 630.304(4) provides that signing a blank prescription form is grounds for
initiating disciplinary action against a licensee.

7. As outlined above, Respondent violated NRS 630.304(4) when he signed nine
blank prescription forms.

8. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.
WHEREFORE, the IC prays:

1. That the Board give Respondent notice of the charges herein against him and give
him notice that he may file an answer to the Complaint herein as set forth in NRS 630.339(2)
within twenty (20) days of service of the Complaint;

2. That the Board set a time and place for a formal hearing after holding an Early
Case Conference pursuant to NRS 630.339(3);

3. That the Board determine what sanctions to impose if it finds and concludes that
there has been a violation or violations of the Medical Practice Act committed by Respondent;

4. That the Board make, issue and serve on Respondent its findings of fact,
conclusions of law and order, in writing, to include sanctions to be imposed; and

5. That the Board take such other and further action as may be just and proper in these
premises.
