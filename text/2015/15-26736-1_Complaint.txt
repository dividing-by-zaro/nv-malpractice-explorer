COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), composed at the time of filing of Theodore B. Berndt, M.D., Valerie J. Clark, BSN,
RHU, LUTCF, and Michael J. Fischer, M.D., by and through Erin L. Albright, Esq., General
Counsel and attorney for the IC, having a reasonable basis to believe that Timothy D. Beckett,
M.D. (Respondent), violated the provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and
Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby
issues its Complaint, stating the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was licensed by the Board, pursuant to the provisions of the Medical
Practice Act, on December 23, 2002, and is currently licensed in active status (License No.
10395).

2. On August 9, 2010, Respondent was arrested by the Las Vegas Metropolitan Police
Department for domestic violence.

3. In 2011, Respondent renewed his license to practice medicine in the state of
Nevada. On his renewal application, Respondent failed to disclose that he was arrested for
domestic violence on August 9, 2010.

Count I

4, All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

5. NRS 630.304(1) provides that obtaining, maintaining or renewing or attempting to
obtain, maintain or renew a license to practice medicine by bribery, fraud or misrepresentation or
by any false, misleading, inaccurate or incomplete statement is grounds for initiating discipline
against a licensee.

6. By failing to disclose the arrest on August 9, 2010 on his Nevada medical license
renewal application, Respondent was obtaining, maintaining or renewing or attempting to obtain,
maintain or renew his license to practice medicine by fraud, misrepresentation or any false,
misleading, inaccurate or incomplete statement.

7. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

Count IT

8. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

9. NRS 630.306(2)(a) provides that engaging in any conduct which is intended to
deceive is grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

10. Respondent’s aforementioned activities constitute conduct which was intended to
deceive.

11. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

1. That the Board give Respondent notice of the charges herein against him and give
him notice that he may file an answer to the Complaint herein as set forth in NRS 630.339(2)
within twenty (20) days of service of the Complaint;

2. That the Board set a time and place for a formal hearing after holding an
