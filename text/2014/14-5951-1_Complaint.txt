COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), composed at the time of filing the formal Complaint of Theodore B. Berndt, M.D.,
Chairman, Valerie J. Clark, BSN, RHU, LUTCF, Member, and Michael J. Fischer, M.D.,
Member, by and through Erin L. Albright, Esq., Board General Counsel and attorney for the IC,
having a reasonable basis to believe that Henry R. Landsman, M.D. (Respondent), has violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code
(NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its formal Complaint,
stating the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent is currently licensed in active status (License No. 4021), and has been
licensed by the Board since March 29, 1980 pursuant to the provisions of the Medical Practice
Act.

2. On or about April 13, 1981, the Medical Board of California (California Board)
issued Physician’s and Surgeon’s Certificate No. G44389 to Respondent.

3. On or about December 1, 2011, the California Board revoked Respondent’s license
to practice medicine in the state of California. The revocation was stayed and Respondent was

placed on probation for five (5) years subject to terms and conditions. Respondent failed to report
this action, in writing, to the Board within thirty (30) days of the order.

4. On May 11, 2012, the California Board issued a Citation Order on Respondent
directing him to provide proof of compliance with the terms and conditions of his probation.
Respondent failed to provide proof of compliance with the terms and conditions of his probation.

5. On or about September 24, 2012, the California Board issued a Cease Practice
Order to Respondent and suspended his license to practice medicine in the state of California due
to Respondent’s failure to provide proof of compliance with the terms and conditions of his
probation. Respondent failed to report this action, in writing, to the Board within thirty (30) days
of the order.

6. On May 15, 2013, the California Board revoked Respondent’s license to practice
medicine in the state of California due to his failure to provide proof of compliance with the terms
and conditions of his probation. Respondent failed to report this action, in writing, to the Board
within thirty (30) days of the revocation.

7. On April 2, 2013, Respondent renewed his license to practice medicine in the state
of Nevada. On this renewal application, Respondent failed to disclose the investigation and/or
formal actions taken against his California medical license by the California Board as required by
law.

Count I

8. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

9. NRS 630.304(1) provides that obtaining, maintaining or renewing or attempting to
obtain, maintain or renew a license to practice medicine by bribery, fraud or misrepresentation or
by any false, misleading, inaccurate or incomplete statement is grounds for initiating discipline
against a licensee.

10. ‘By failing to disclose the investigation and/or formal actions taken against his
California medical license by the California Board on his Nevada medical license renewal

application, Respondent was obtaining, maintaining or renewing or attempting to obtain, maintain

Count IV

20. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

21. NRS 630.301(3) provides that any disciplinary action taken by another state is
grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

22. The various disciplinary actions taken by the California Board outlined above is
grounds for initiating discipline against Respondent.

23. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

1. That the Board give Respondent notice of the charges herein against him and give

2. That the Board set a time and place for a formal hearing after holding an Early

3, That the Board determine what sanctions it agrees upon to impose if it finds and

Respondent; and

4. That the Board make, issue and serve on Respondent its findings of facts
