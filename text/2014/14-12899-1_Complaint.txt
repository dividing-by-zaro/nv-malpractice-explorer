COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), composed, at the time of filing, of Theodore B. Berndt, M.D., Valerie J. Clark,
BSN, RHU, LUTCF, and Michael J. Fischer, M.D., by and through Bradley O. Van Ry, Esq.,
Generai Counsel and attorney for the IC, having a reasonable basis to believe that
Ronald Foote, M.D. (Respondent), violated the provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS)
Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively, the MPA),
hereby issues its formal Complaint, stating the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent is currently licensed to practice medicine in the state of Nevada
(License No. 9240), and has been so licensed by the Board since December 4, 1999, pursuant to
the provisions of the MPA.

2. On or about May 30, 2014, the IC suspended Respondent’s license to practice
medicine in the state of Nevada by written stipulation with Respondent pursuant to
NRS 630.326. Accordingly, Respondent’s license to practice medicine in the state of Nevada
remains indefinitely suspended.

3. On or about March 17, 2006, the Board entered a Findings of Fact, Conclusions of

Law and Order (2006 FOFCLO) in Case No. 05-12899-31 involving Respondent. In that

‘FOFCLO, Respondent was found in violation of NRS 630.301(9). Respondent was ordered,

among other things, to continue and remain compliant with, and completely participate in, the

Board recognized diversion program that he was enrolled in at the time of entry of the 2006

FOFCLO. Those obligations were long-term and effective until June 16, 2010.

4, On or about August 11, 2009, the Board filed a Settlement Agreement (2009
Settlement Agreement) with Respondent. In the 2009 Settlement Agreement, Respondent agreed
that he violated NRS 630.304(1). Respondent agreed to, among other things, a stayed suspension
of his license to practice medicine in the state of Nevada for one year, and probation for a like
term with a condition that Respondent obey all laws and regulations during the term of probation.

5. On or about March 14, 2011, the Board filed another Settlement Agreement (2011
Settlement Agreement) with Respondent. In that Settlement Agreement, Respondent agreed that
he violated the 2006 FOFCLO and the 2009 Settlement Agreement in violation of NRS
630.3065(2){a). Respondent additionally agreed to, among other things, a stayed revocation of his
license to practice medicine in the state of Nevada. The stayed revocation was conditioned upon
probation for the entire period of his continued compliance with, and complete participation in,
the Board recognized diversion program with what is now the Nevada Professionals Assistance
Program (NPAP) for a period of five years beginning June 18, 2010 and until such time as he was
cleared to exit such program.

6. Under the terms of the NPAP program, Respondent was to “completely abstain
from alcohol. ...” for the entire term of his NPAP program.

7. On or about April 28, 2014, at 12:59 a.m., Respondent was arrested for driving
under the influence. During the arrest, Respondent admitted that “I had a bottle of wine.”

8. At the time of the arrest, a blood sample was taken from Respondent and sent to the
Las Vegas Metropolitan Police Department Forensic Laboratory for analysis. The analysis
confirmed that Respondent had been drinking. Respondent had a blood alcohol concentration of
0.117 g/100 ml at the time of the arrest.

9. By drinking any alcohol on April 28, 2014, Respondent violated another Board
order for the third time since the 2006 FOFCLO.

COUNT I
(Willful Failure to Comply With an Order of the Board)

10. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

11. NRS 630.3065(2)(a) provides that the willful failure to comply with an order of the
Board is grounds for initiating disciplinary action against a licensee.

12. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent's acts
in violating the 2006 FOFCLO, the 2009 Settlement Agreement and the 2011 Settlement
Agreement were all willful failures to comply with an order of the Board in violation of
NRS 630.3065(2)(a).

13. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as

‘provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT If
(Disrepute)

14. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

15. NRS 630.301(9) provides that engaging in conduct that brings the medical
profession into disrepute is grounds for initiating disciplinary action against a licensee.

16. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent's acts
in violating the 2006 FOFCLO, the 2009 Settlement Agreement and the 2011 Settlement
Agreement, as well as getting arrested for driving under the influence, were engaging in conduct
that brought the medical profession into disrepute in violation of NRS 630.301(9).

17, By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.
