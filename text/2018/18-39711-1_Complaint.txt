COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners,
hereby issues this formal Complaint against Dhiraj Raj Jeyanandarajan, M.D. (hereinafter referred
to as Respondent), a licensed physician in Nevada. After investigating this matter, the IC has a
reasonable basis to believe that Respondent has violated provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes
(NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively, the
Medical Practice Act).

The IC alleges the following facts:

L. Respondent is currently licensed in active status (License No. 14512). Respondent
was issued his license from the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners on August 16, 2012,
pursuant to the provisions of NRS Chapter 630.

2. On or about September 6, 2017, the State Board of Medicine of the Commonwealth
of Pennsylvania entered into a Consent Agreement and Order with Respondent, whereby
Respondent was disciplined for failing to comply with continuing education requirements to
renew his medical license in Pennsylvania; Respondent was 31.0 hours short of the minimum one
hundred (100) total hours of continuing medical education required for renewal of his license.

3. Respondent did not timely report the September 6, 2017 Consent Agreement and

Order to the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners.

Complaint was approved, was composed of Wayne Hardwick, M.D., Mr. M. Neil Duxbury, and Aury Nagy, MLD.

5. NRS 630.301(3) provides that any disciplinary action, including the revocation,
suspension, modification or limitation of a license to practice any type of medicine, taken by
another state, is grounds for initiating disciplinary action against a licensee.

6. On or about September 6, 2017, the State Board of Medicine of the Commonwealth

comply with the Pennsylvania continuing medical education requirements.

7. The September 6, 2017 Consent Agreement and Order was a disciplinary action taken
by another state.

8. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NRS 630.352.

10. NRS 630.306(1)(k) provides that failure by a licensee or applicant to report in
writing, within 30 days, any disciplinary action taken against the licensee or applicant by another
state, the Federal Government or a foreign country, including, without limitation, the revocation,
suspension or surrender of a license to practice medicine in another jurisdiction, is grounds for
initiating disciplinary action.

11. | Respondent was disciplined by the State Board of Medicine of the Commonwealth
of Pennsylvania as described above.

12. Respondent failed to timely report the disciplinary action taken by the State Board of
Medicine of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania.

13. Accordingly, Respondent has violated NRS 630.306(1)(k).

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

1. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners give Respondent notice of the

2. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners set a time and place for a
formal hearing after holding an Early Case Conference pursuant to NRS 630.339(3);

impose if it determines there has been a violation or violations of the Medical Practice Act
committed by Respondent;

Respondent its findings of fact, conclusions of law and order, in writing, that includes the
