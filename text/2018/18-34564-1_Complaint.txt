COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners (Board)
hereby issues this formal Complaint (Complaint) against Lindsay Marie Hoffman, PA-C
(Respondent), a physician assistant licensed in Nevada. After investigating this matter, the IC has a
reasonable basis to believe that Respondent has violated provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes
(NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical

Practice Act). The IC alleges the following facts:

A. Respondent’s Violation of Nevada Pharmacy Laws.

1. Respondent is a physician assistant licensed to practice medicine in the State of
Nevada (License No. PA1154). She has been continuously licensed by the Board since
January 22, 2009.

2. At all times relevant to this Complaint, Respondent was the supervisee and

employee of Ivan Lee Goldsmith, M.D.

3. At the direction of Dr. Goldsmith and/or upon Respondent’s own accord,
Respondent dispensed controlled substances and dangerous drugs to patients of Dr. Goldsmith,
from Dr. Goldsmith’s store of medications, while Dr. Goldsmith was out of the country and

otherwise not on-site at the dispensing facility.

8. NAC 639.945(1) provides, in pertinent part, that the following acts or practices by

a holder of any license, certificate or registration issued by the Pharmacy Board or any employee

of any business holding any such license, certificate or registration are declared to be, specifically
but not by way of limitation, unprofessional conduct and conduct contrary to the public interest:

(i) Performing any of his or her duties as the holder of a license, certificate or

registration issued by the Board, or as the owner of a business or an entity licensed

by the Board, in an incompetent, unskillful or negligent manner.

(n) Dispensing a drug as a dispensing practitioner to a patient with whom the
dispensing practitioner does not have a bona fide therapeutic relationship.
(o) Prescribing a drug as a prescribing practitioner to a patient with whom the
prescribing practitioner does not have a bona fide therapeutic relationship.

9. NAC. 639.945(3) provides that, for purposes of applying Pharmacy Board
regulations, a bona fide therapeutic relationship between the patient and practitioner shall be
deemed to exist if the patient was examined in person, electronically or telephonically by the
practitioner within the 6 months immediately preceding the date the practitioner dispenses or
prescribes a drug to the patient and, as a result of the examination, the practitioner diagnosed a

condition for which a given drug therapy is prescribed.

Count I
NRS 630.306(1)(c) (Unauthorized Dispensing)
10. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by

reference as though fully set forth herein.
11. Pursuant to NRS 630.306(1)(c), dispensing any controlled substance, or any

dangerous drug as defined in chapter 454 of NRS, to others except as authorized by law is grounds

for discipline.

12. Pursuant to NAC 630.380(1)(m), violating a provision of NRS 630.301 to

630.3065, inclusive, is grounds for discipline.

13. Pursuant to NAC 630.380(1)(h), dispensing any controlled substance otherwise
than as authorized by law and the supervising physician of the physician assistant is grounds for
discipline.

14. By dispensing controlled substances and dangerous drugs to Dr. Goldsmith’s
patients from Dr. Goldsmith’s store of medications when Dr. Goldsmith was not on-site at his
medical facility, Respondent violated NRS 639.742 and NAC 639.945(1).

15. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

Count II
NRS 630.306(1)(c) (Unauthorized Prescribing)

16. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

17. Pursuant to NRS 630.306(1)(c), prescribing any controlled substance, or any
dangerous drug as defined in chapter 454 of NRS, to others except as authorized by law is grounds
for discipline.

18. Pursuant to NAC 630.380(1)(m), violating a provision of NRS 630.301 to
630.3065, inclusive, is grounds for discipline.

19. By prescribing medications to Dr. Goldsmith’s patients without a bona fide
therapeutic relationship with said patients, Respondent violated NAC 639.945.

20. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

Count Ill
NRS 630.3062(1)(a) (Failure to Maintain Complete Medical Records)

21. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

22. NRS 630.3062(1)(a) provides that the failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate
and complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient is grounds

for initiating discipline against a licensee.
