COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(“Board”) hereby issues this formal Complaint against Russell P. Gollard, M.D. (hereinafter
referred to as Respondent), a licensed physician in Nevada. After investigating this matter, the IC
has a reasonable basis to believe that Respondent has violated provisions of Nevada Revised
Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively,
the Medical Practice Act).

The IC alleges the following facts:

l. Respondent is currently licensed in active status (License No. 7818). Respondent

was issued his license from the Board on June 12, 1996, pursuant to the provisions of NRS

Chapter 630.

2. Patient A was a 41-year-old female at the time of the events at issue. Her true
identity is not disclosed herein to protect her privacy, but is disclosed in the Patient Designation

served upon Respondent along with a copy of this Complaint.

3. On or about October 12, 2011, Patient A first presented to Respondent for

treatment for breast cancer.

4, Respondent performed a clinical assessment of Patient A and determined that
Patient A was “DCIS, high-grade, ER/PR negative.”
5. Respondent started Patient A on a chemotherapy regimen of Taxotere and Cytoxan,

based on the clinical assessment.

6. In or about December 2011, Respondent recommended four (4) cycles of

chemotherapy (Taxotere and Cytoxan).

7. Patient A received treatment from in or about October 2011 until in or about March
2015.

8. Patient A’s medical records did not include the following: clinical facts, whether
necessary informed consents were performed on a regular basis, discussions with Patient A
regarding pathology, prognosis, treatment options, and the toxicity and side effects of therapeutic
interventions.

COUNT I
NRS 630.3062(1)(a) (Failure to Maintain Proper Medical Records)

9, All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by reference
as though fully set forth herein.

10. NRS 630.3062(1)(a) provides that the failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate
and complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient constitutes
grounds for initiating disciplinary action.

11. Respondent failed to maintain complete medical records relating to the diagnosis,
treatment and care of Patient A, by failing to sufficiently document the following: clinical facts,
whether necessary informed consents were performed on a regular basis, documentation of
discussions with Patient A regarding pathology, prognosis, treatment options, and the toxicity and
side effects of therapeutic interventions.

12. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:
