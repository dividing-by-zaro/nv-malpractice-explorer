FIRST AMENDED COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee’ (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Aaron Bart Fricke, J.D., General Counsel and attorney for the IC, having a
reasonable basis to believe that Theodore Melvin Thorp, M.D., (Respondent) violated the provisions
of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC)
Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its First Amended Complaint
(Complaint), stating the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a medical doctor holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 2979). Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on July 1, 1975.

2. At all times relevant to this Complaint, Respondent was the supervising physician
responsible for the medical activities of Robert Joseph Webb, PA, License No. 434 (Webb), a
physician assistant licensed by the Board, pursuant to a formal notice of supervision provided to
the Board on or about August 27, 2012, and filed in accord with NAC 630.360(4), wherein
Respondent certified that he had read and was aware of all provisions of NRS Chapter 630 and
NAC Chapter 630 concerning his duties and professional responsibilities as a physician

3. Pursuant to NAC 630.370, at all times relevant to this Complaint, as Webb’s
supervising physician, Respondent was responsible for all the medical activities of Webb.

4. Pursuant to NAC 630.375, at all times relevant to this Complaint, Webb was
considered to be and was deemed the agent of Respondent in the performance of all medical

5. At all times relevant to this Complaint, Webb owned, operated and/or served as the
medical director of CIMA Medical Center, located at 1321 S Rainbow Blvd., Suite 101,
Las Vegas, NV 89146 (CIMA Rainbow).

6. At all times relevant to this Complaint, Webb owned, operated and/or served as the
medical director of Modern Wellness Clinic, located at 1321 S Rainbow Blvd., Suite 280,
Las Vegas, NV 89146 (MWC).

7. At all times relevant to this Complaint, Webb owned, operated and/or served as the
medical director of CIMA Medical Center, located at 2354 E. Bonanza Road, Las Vegas,
NV 89101 (CIMA Bonanza).

8. Webb’s son, Robert Webb, Jr., was the operations and marketing manager of
MWC. Robert Webb, Jr., is not a licensed medical practitioner as defined by NRS 639.0125.

9. Webb permitted Robert Webb, Jr., and/or other unauthorized persons, to act under
his authority to operate CIMA Bonanza, CIMA Rainbow and MWC to purchase, access, store,
possess, administer, furnish, prescribe and/or dispense controlled substances and/or dangerous
drugs to patients with whom Webb had not established a bona fide therapeutic relationship, before
Webb had examined the patients and without his direct supervision, and for whom Webb had
neither diagnosed nor determined that a controlled substance or dangerous drug was medically
necessary.

10. Webb unlawfully issued one hundred eighty-four (184) prescriptions for controlled
substances and three hundred ninety-four (394) prescriptions for dangerous drugs while traveling

outside of the U.S. from July 8-14, 2017, July 21-August 2, 2017, and March 1-6, 2018, by

14. All controlled substances and dangerous drugs in the possession of a physician

15. Performing or in any way being a party to any fraudulent or deceitful practice or

to NAC 639.945(1)(h) and is grounds for suspension or revocation of any license or registration
issued by the Pharmacy Board. See NRS 639.210(4).

16. Performing any duties as the holder of a controlled substance registration or a
dispensing practitioner registration in an incompetent, unskillful or negligent manner constitutes
unprofessional conduct or conduct contrary to the public interest pursuant to NAC 639.945(1)(i)
and is grounds for suspension or revocation of any license or registration issued by the Pharmacy
Board. See NRS 639.210(4).

17. Aiding or abetting a person not licensed to practice pharmacy in the State of
Nevada constitutes unprofessional conduct or conduct contrary to the public interest pursuant to
NAC 639.945(1)(j) and is grounds for suspension or revocation of any license or registration
issued by the Pharmacy Board. See NRS 639.210(4).

COUNT I
NAC 630.230(1)(i) — Failure to Provide Adequate Supervision of a Physician Assistant

21. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

22. Pursuant to NAC 630.230(1)(i), it is a standard of practice that a physician shall
not fail to provide adequate supervision of a physician assistant.

23. Pursuant to NRS 630.301(1)(b)(2), engaging in any conduct which the Board has
determined is a violation of the standards of practice established by regulation of the Board is
grounds for initiating disciplinary action or denying licensure.

24. Demonstrated by but not limited to the conduct described herein, Respondent failed
to provide adequate supervision of his physician assistant, Webb.

25. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NRS 630.352.
