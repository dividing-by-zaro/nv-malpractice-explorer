COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(“Board”) hereby issues this formal Complaint against Muhammad A. Syed, M.D. (hereinafter
referred to as Respondent), a licensed physician in Nevada. After investigating this matter, the IC
has a reasonable basis to believe that Respondent has violated provisions of Nevada Revised
Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively,
the Medical Practice Act).

The IC alleges the following facts:

1. Respondent is currently, and was at all times relevant to this Complaint, licensed in
active status (License No. 11648). Respondent was initially issued his license from the Board on
October 5, 2005, pursuant to the provisions of NRS Chapter 630.

3. On August 4, 2016, Patient A first presented to Respondent at Sunrise Hospital and
Medical Center (Sunrise Hospital), complaining of left lower extremity weakness. Respondent
examined Patient A and documented her medical history.

4. On August 18, 2016, Respondent accepted Patient A for admission into Sunrise
Hospital and performed another physical examination and documented the history of Patient A,

which included various comorbidities.

5. Patient A was also seen by various other providers during her stay at Sunrise
Hospital.

6. On August 23, 2016, Respondent discharged Patient A from Sunrise Hospital to
Las Vegas Post-Acute and Rehabilitation (LVPAR), a skilled facility. The discharge records

issued by Respondent were incomplete, failed to acknowledge, document, communicate and
coordinate services necessary for the continued care of Patient A. Additionally, Respondent did
not consult and coordinate Patient A’s continued care with a provider at LVPAR.

7. After a brief stay at LVPAR, Patient A was discharged from the facility to her
home where she passed away on September 17, 2016.

COUNTI
NRS 630.304(7) (Terminating the Medical Care of a Patient Without Making Other
Arrangements for the Continued Care of the Patient)

8 _Alll of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by reference
as though fully set forth herein.

9. NRS 630.304(7) provides that terminating the medical care of a patient without
making other arrangements for the continued care of the patient constitutes grounds for initiating
disciplinary action against a licensee.

10. Respondent discharged Patient A from Sunrise Hospital and sent her to LVPAR.
Respondent's discharge records did not adequately capture the other hospital consultant
assessments, diagnostics, treatment, and quality of continuation of care upon transferring Patient
Ato LVPAR.

COUNTII
NRS 630.3062(1)(a) (Failure to Maintain Proper Medical Records)

12. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by reference
as though fully set forth herein.

13. NRS 630.3062(1)(a) provides that the failure to maintain complete medical records
relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient constitutes grounds for initiating
disciplinary action against a licensee.

14, Respondent failed to maintain complete medical records relating to the diagnosis,
treatment and care of Patient A when he failed to completely document the coordination of|
services relating to the current and ongoing care of Patient A.

15. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.
