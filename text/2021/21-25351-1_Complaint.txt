COMPLAINT ———

The Investigative Committee (IC)' of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Aaron Bart Fricke, Esq., Senior Deputy General Counsel and attorney for
the IC, having a reasonable basis to believe that Kristin Adele Hestdalen, M.D., (Respondent),
violated the provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and the Nevada
Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its
Complaint (Complaint), stating the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a licensed medical doctor
holding an active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 10215).
Respondent was originally licensed by the Board on July 1, 2002.

2. At all times relevant to this Complaint, Respondent held either an active or expired
Nevada Controlled Substance Registration, Certificate No. CS I 1061, issued by the Nevada State
Board of Pharmacy (Pharmacy Board).

3. Respondent failed to timely renew her Certificate of Registration No. CS 11061,
issued by the Pharmacy Board, which expired on October 31, 2018.

4. Respondent wrote two hundred sixty-three (263) prescriptions for controlled
substances between November 1, 2018 and May 3, 2019.

5. The Pharmacy Board approved a renewed Controlled Substance Registration for
Respondent on December 4, 2019, at which time Respondent appeared before the Pharmacy
Board to answer questions and give testimony regarding her application and the facts and
circumstances the foregoing.

6. Pursuant to a Stipulation and Order entered into between the Respondent and the
Pharmacy Board on June 10, 2020, Respondent admitted to and was disciplined by the Pharmacy
Board for violation of NRS 453.226(1), NRS 453.321(1)(a), NRS 639.100(1) and
NRS 639.2813(1), whereby she received a letter of reprimand from the Pharmacy Board, and was
ordered to pay a fine of five thousand dollars ($5,000.00), among other conditions of disciplinary
action.

COUNT I
NRS 630.306(1)(b)(3)

(Violation of Statutes and Regulations of the Nevada State Board of Pharmacy)

7. All of the allegations contained in Paragraphs 1-6 above are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

8. Respondent is a practitioner as defined by NRS 639.0125(1), as a physician, who
holds a license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada.

9. NRS 630.306(1)(b)(3) provides that engaging in conduct that violates a provision
of chapter 639 of NRS, or a regulation adopted by the State Board of Pharmacy pursuant thereto,
that is applicable to a licensee who is a practitioner, as defined in NRS 639.0125, is grounds for
initiating discipline against a licensee.

10. Respondent violated NRS 639.100(1) and NRS 639.2813(1).

11. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

1. That the Board give Respondent notice of the charges herein against her and give
her notice that she may file an answer to the Complaint herein as set forth in NRS 630.339(2)

within twenty (20) days of service of the Complaint;
