COMPLAINT
The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Robert G. Kilroy, Esq., General Counsel and attomey for the IC, having a
reasonable basis to believe that Sajjad Ejaz Komboj, M.D. (Respondent) violated the pro
Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630

ions of |

(collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating the IC’s charges and
allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a Medical Doctor holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 9203). Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on October 20, 1999.

2. On June 28, 2018, Patient A? presented to Respondent for an electrocardiogram.
Due to a disagreement between the Patient A and Respondent, this patient encounter did not result
in any medical care being provided by Respondent. Subsequently, on July 2, 2018, Patient A
reported a grievance against Respondent regarding his demeanor and his staffs’ conduct.

3. On August 14, 2018, the IC issued an Order to Produce Health Care Records to the
Respondent regarding his encounter and treatment of Patient A and an allegation letter seeking his.
response to the allegations contained therein.

4. The IC attempted multiple times to communicate with Respondent about disclosure
of the requested medical records. After several unproductive conversations with Respondent and
Respondent’s staff, who continually asserted the medical records of Patient A were in route to be
delivered to the IC, it was disclosed that no records actually exist for Patient A.

5. On February 18, 2020, Respondent informed the IC that he had responded to the
allegation letter and that there were no medical records for Patient A. Respondent informed the IC,
more than five hundred thirty-two (532) days after the deadline for responding to the Board’s Order
for records, that he was unable to produce any medical records for Patient A.

COUNTI
NRS 630.3065(2)(a) (Failure to Comply with a Board Order)

6. _ Allofthe allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by reference as
though fully set forth herein.

7. On August 14, 2018, the IC sent Respondent an Order to Produce Medical Records
which provided twenty-one (21) days to respond with medical records for the subject patient.

8. Respondent did not send Patient A’s medical records within the allotted twenty-one
(21) days.

9. NRS 630.3065(2)(a) provides that the knowing or willful failure to comply with an
order of the Board constitutes grounds for initiating disciplinary action.

10. Respondent failed to comply with an Order of the Board when he failed to advise the
existence and/or location of the subject Patient A’s medical records to the IC.

11. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT IL
NRS 630.3062(1)(a) - (Failure to Maintain Complete Medical Records)

12, All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

13, NRS 630,3062(1)(a) provides that the failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate
and complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient is grounds
for initiating disciplinary action against a licensee.

14, Respondent failed to maintain complete medical records relating to the diagnosis,
treatment and care of Patient A, by failing to document his interactions with Patient A on
June 28, 2018. Patient A’s medical records were not timely, legible, accurate, and complete,
because Respondent did not document anything regarding this patient encounter.

15. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

1. That the Board give Respondent notice of the charges herein against him and give
him notice that he may file an answer to the Complaint herein as set forth in NRS 630.339(2)
within twenty (20) days of service of the Complaint;

2. That the Board set a time and place for a formal hearing after holding an Early
Case Conference pursuant to NRS 630.339(3);

3. That the Board determine what sanctions to impose if it determines there has been
a violation or violations of the Medical Practice Act committed by Respondent;

4, That the Board make, issue and serve on Respondent its findings of fact,
conclusions of law and order, in writing, that includes the sanctions imposed;

3. That the Board awards such fees and costs outlined in NRS 622.400 for the
investigation and prosecution of this disciplinary action; and
