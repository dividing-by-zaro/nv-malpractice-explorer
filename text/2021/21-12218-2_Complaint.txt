COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Aaron Bart Fricke, J.D., General Counsel and attorney for the IC, having a
reasonable basis to believe that Rajeev Sharad Khamamkar, M.D., (Respondent) violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC)
Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating the IC’s
charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a medical doctor holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 8597). Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on April 8, 1998.

2. On November 19, 2020, Respondent’s clinical privileges at Specialty Surgery
Center, 7250 Cathedral Rock Dr. Las Vegas, NV 89128, were summarily suspended, indefinitely,
pending hearing and appellate review process, because of concerns regarding his compliance with
Specialty Surgery Center policies, procedures and practices. Hearing and appellate review
processes were completed and the following action was taken: Respondent was ordered to
complete approved professional courses on anger management, proper billing practices and

maintaining safety of controlled substances and to remain on suspension until the successful

3. This ‘“‘adverse action” by Specialty Surgery Center was a sanction imposed on
Respondent that is reportable to the National Practitioner Databank (NPDB) pursuant to Title IV
of Public Law 99-660, “The Health Care Quality Improvement Act of 1986,” as amended
42 USC Sec. 11101, January 26, 1998.

4. Specialty Surgery Center is a “health care entity’ as defined by
42 USC Sec. 11151(4)(a)(2), and, accordingly, properly reported this adverse action to the NPDB
on or about December 8, 2020.

5. Pursuant to NRS 630.3068(1)(d), a physician is required to report to the Board
“fa]ny sanctions imposed against the physician that are reportable to the National Practitioner
Data Bank not later than 45 days after the sanctions are imposed.” See NRS 630.3068(1)(d).

6. Pursuant to NRS 630.3068(2), if the Board finds that a physician has violated any
provision of NRS 630.3068, the Board may impose a fine of not more than $5,000 against the
physician for each violation, in addition to any other fines or penalties permitted by law.

7. Respondent did not report to the Board this adverse action within forty-five (45)
days after the sanction was imposed.

COUNT I
NRS 630.3062(1)(e) — Failure to Report Sanction Reportable to NPDB

8. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

9. Pursuant to NRS 630.3062(1)(e), the failure of a physician to comply with the
requirements of NRS 630.3068 is grounds for initiating disciplinary action or denying licensure.

10. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent
violated NRS 630.3068(1)(d) by failing to report the adverse action taken on November 19, 2020
by Specialty Surgery Center, within forty-five (45) days of the action.

11. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as

provided in NRS 630.352.
