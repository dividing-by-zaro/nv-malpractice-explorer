COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Aaron Bart Fricke, J.D., General Counsel and attorney for the IC, having
a reasonable basis to believe that Vivian Ann Hendrix, CRT, (Respondent) violated the provisions
of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter
630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating the IC’s charges
and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a certified respiratory
therapist holding an active license to practice respiratory therapy in the State of Nevada
(License No. RC165). Respondent was originally licensed by the Board on November 29, 2001.

COUNTI
NRS 630.306(1)(b)(1) — Deceptive Conduct

2. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

3. Pursuant to NAC 630.540, a practitioner of respiratory care is subject to discipline
or denial of licensure by the Board if, after notice and hearing in accordance with this chapter, the

Board finds that the practitioner of respiratory care violated, among other provisions, any

provision that would subject a practitioner of medicine to discipline pursuant to NRS 630.301 to

4. Pursuant to NRS 630.306(1)(b)(1), engaging in conduct, which is intended to
deceive, is grounds for initiating disciplinary action or denying licensure.

5. On October 3, 2015, Respondent was employed at University Medical Center at
1800 W Charleston Blvd, Las Vegas, NV 89102 (UMC) as a respiratory therapist. She clocked-in
for work that day at UMC at 5:26 a.m.

6. Respondent then left UMC, and crossed Charleston Boulevard and entered
St. Joseph Transitional Rehabilitation Center at 2035 W Charleston Blvd, Las Vegas, NV 89102
(SJTRC), where she was also employed as a respiratory therapist. Respondent clocked-in for
work at SJTRC at 6:02 a.m. that same day.

7. Respondent then returned to UMC and clocked-out at 12:02 p.m. the same day.

8. Respondent then clocked-in at UMC at 12:32 p.m. the same day.

9. Respondent then returned to SJTRC and clocked-out at 1:00 p.m. the same day.

10. Respondent then clocked-out at SJTRC at 2:45 p.m. the same day.

11. Respondent then returned to UMC and clocked-out at 5:54 p.m. the same day.

12. Again, on November 14, 2015, Respondent was employed at UMC as a respiratory
therapist. She clocked-in for work that day at UMC at 5:26 a.m.

13. Respondent then left UMC, and again crossed Charleston Boulevard and entered
SJTRC, where she was also employed as a respiratory therapist. Respondent clocked-in for work
at SJTRC at 5:58 a.m. that same day.

14. Respondent then clocked-out at SJTRC at 1:31 p.m. the same day.

15. Respondent then clocked-in at SJTRC at 2:01 p.m. the same day.

16. Respondent then clocked-out at SJTRC at 2:23 p.m. the same day.

17. Respondent then returned to UMC and clocked-out at 3:00 p.m. the same day.

18. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent
engaged in conduct intended to deceive both her employers, UMC and SJTRC, respectively, on

both October 3, 2015, and on November 14, 2015, by clocking-in to work and claiming that she

Early Case Conference pursuant to NRS 630.339(3);

4. That the Board award fees and costs for the investigation and prosecution of this
