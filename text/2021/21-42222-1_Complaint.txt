COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Aaron Bart Fricke, J.D., General Counsel and attorney for the IC, having a
reasonable basis to believe that David Victor Ludlow, M.D., (Respondent) violated the provisions of
Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630
(collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating the IC’s charges and
allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a medical doctor holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 15226). Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on March 21, 2014.

2. Pursuant to NRS 630.3062(1)(h), fraudulent, illegal, unauthorized or otherwise
inappropriate prescribing, administering or dispensing of a controlled substance listed in schedule II,
III or IV, is grounds for initiating disciplinary action or denying licensure.

3. NRS 630.3062 provides that failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate and
complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient is grounds for
initiating disciplinary action or denying licensure. _


' The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, at the time this formal
Complaint was authorized for filing, was composed of Board members Mr. M. Neil Duxbury, Aury Nagy, M.D., and
Michael C. Edwards, M.D., FACS.

COUNT I
NRS 630.3062(1)(h) — Unauthorized and Otherwise Inappropriate Prescribing
of a Schedule II Controlled Substance

4. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

5. Respondent, a urologist, prescribed Adderall, a schedule II controlled substance, in
an unauthorized and otherwise inappropriate manner when he issued at least twenty-one (21)
prescriptions to Patient A for Dextroamp-Amphetamin, 30 mg tablets, from July 2018 through
June 2020 (e.g., on July 16, 2018, August 15, 2018, September 13, 2018, October 11, 2018,
November 9, 2018, December 10, 2018, January 11, 2019, February 8, 2019, March 11, 2019,
April 29, 2019, May 29, 2019, June 28. 2019, July 29, 2019, August 26, 2019,
September 27, 2019, October 24, 2019, December 2, 2019, January 31, 2020, March 9, 2020,
April 20, 2020, and June 15, 2020) without documenting any patient visits, encounters,
examinations, tests or diagnoses of Patient A. Respondent further failed to provide justification
for the medication, failed to monitor the effectiveness of the medication, failed to monitor
Patient A for elevated blood pressure, among other conditions, and prescribed such medication
without first and periodically checking the Nevada Prescription Monitoring Program for signs of
duplicate prescriptions, abuse or diversion, among other failures to satisfy requirements
established by Nevada statutes and regulations.

6. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT II
NRS 630.3062(1)(a) — Failure to Maintain Proper Medical Records

7. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated
by reference as though fully set forth herein.

8. Respondent failed to maintain complete medical records relating to the diagnosis,
treatment and care of Patient A by failing to maintain any records of his diagnosis, treatment and

care of Patient A.
