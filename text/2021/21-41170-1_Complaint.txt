COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Aaron Bart Fricke, J.D., General Counsel and attorney for the IC, having a
reasonable basis to believe that ADELBERT SCOTT WADSWORTH, PA-C (Respondent)
violated the provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative
Code (NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint,
stating the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

i? Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a physician assistant holding
an active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. PA1449). Respondent
was originally licensed by the Board on August 13, 2013.

2. Pursuant to NRS 630.020(1), the “Practice of medicine” means:

1. To diagnose, treat, correct, prevent or prescribe for any human
disease, ailment, injury, infirmity, deformity or other condition,
physical or mental, by any means or instrumentality, including, but
not limited to, the performance of an autopsy. [emphasis added].



' The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, at the time this formal
Complaint was authorized for filing, was composed of Board members Mr. M. Neil Duxbury, Michael C. Edwards,
M.D., FACS, and Aury Nagy, M.D.

3. Pursuant to NAC 630.375(2) a physician assistant shall not perform medical
services without supervision from his or her supervising physician, except in life-threatening and
emergency situations.

4, Pursuant to NAC 630.380(1)(c), performing medical services otherwise than in a
life-threatening or emergency situation, and otherwise than with supervision from a supervising
physician, is grounds for initiating disciplinary action against a physician assistant.

5. NAC 630.380(1)(m) provides that violating any provision of NRS 630.301 to
630.3065, is grounds for initiating disciplinary action against a physician assistant.

6. NRS 630.3062 provides that failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate and
complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient is grounds for
initiating disciplinary action or denying licensure.

COUNT I
NAC 630.380(1)(c) — Performance of Medical Services Without a Supervising Physician

7. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

8. Respondent performed medical services otherwise than in a life-threatening or
emergency situation, and without the supervision of a supervising physician, when, on
July 27, 2020, he wrote a prescription for Ambien 10 mg, QHS, 30 ct., with five (5) refills, for
Patient A? to treat insomnia.

9. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT I
NRS 630.3062(1)(a) — Failure to Maintain Proper Medical Records

10. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated
by reference as though fully set forth herein.
