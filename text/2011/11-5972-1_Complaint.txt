COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, composed

at the time filing of the instant Complaint was approved of Theodore B. Berndt, M.D.,
Ms. Valerie Clark, BSN, RHU, LUTCF, and Michael Fischer, M.D., having a reasonable basis to
believe that Karen Arcotta, M.D., hereinafter referred to as Dr. Arcotta, has violated the provisions
of NRS Chapter 630, hereby issues its formal Complaint, stating the Investigative Committee's
charges and allegations, as follows:

1. Dr. Arcotta is licensed to practice medicine in the state of Nevada
(License No. 4896), and was originally licensed on July 9, 1983 by the Nevada State Board of
Medical Examiners pursuant to the provisions of Chapter 630 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

2, In August 2011, as part of normal protocol, an investigator for the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners had multiple contacts with Dr. Arcotta as part of an on-going
investigation. In the course of these contacts, the investigator witnessed erratic behavior on the
part of Dr. Arcotta, Such erratic behavior had also been reported to the investigator by former
co-workers of Dr. Arcotta, including one incident involving a physical altercation with another
staff member.

3. In the course of the on-going investigation, Dr. Arcotta was ordered to submit to a
psychiatric evaluation by the Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical
Examiners pursuant to Nevada Revised Statute (NRS) Section 630.318.

4. The findings of the ordered evaluation indicated that Dr. Arcotta is currently not
able to safely practice medicine and based on a review of all available information, the
Investigative Committee issued its Order summarily suspending the license to practice medicine of
Dr. Arcotta pursuant to NRS 630.326(1) on September 22, 2011.

5. During the course of the investigation, it was discovered that on March 16, 2011,
Dr. Arcotta was convicted of misdemeanor Driving Under the Influence in Las Vegas Township
Justice Court for an arrest made on November 23, 2009. Dr. Arcotta failed to timely report the
conviction to the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners.

6. On June 26, 2011, Dr. Arcotta renewed her license to practice medicine in the state
of Nevada which asks for information for the time period since the previous license renewal
period. Dr. Arcotta answered negatively to question #6 which asks in part “[h]ave you been
arrested, investigated for, charged with, convicted or pled guilty or nolo contendere to any offense
or violation of federal...state, or local law...which is a misdemeanor, gross misdemeanor,
felony...excluding any minor traffic offense (driving or being in control of a motor vehicle while
under the influence of a chemical substance, including alcohol, is not considered a minor traffic
offense)” and failed to disclose either the November 2009 arrest or the March 2011 conviction
when renewing her license.

7. The license to practice medicine of Dr. Arcotta remains suspended at the time of
the filing of this Complaint.

Count I

8. NRS 630.306(13) provides that failure to be found competent to practice medicine
as a result of an examination to determine medical competency pursuant to NRS 630.318 is
grounds for initiating disciplinary action against a physician.

9. Dr. Arcotta was found to be unable to safely practice medicine after submitting to a
psychiatric evaluation order by the Investigative Committee pursuant to NRS 630,318 and
accordingly is in violation of NRS 630.306(13).

10. By reason of the foregoing, Dr. Arcotta is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NRS 630.352.

Count II

11. NRS 630.306(12) provides that failure of a licensee to report in writing, within
30 days, any criminal action taken or conviction obtained against the licensee, other than a minor
traffic violation in this State, or any other state, is grounds for initiating disciplinary action against
a physician.

12. Dr. Arcotta failed to report to the Board her March 16, 2011 conviction for a
misdemeanor Driving Under the Influence in Las Vegas Township Justice Court within 30 days of
the conviction and to do has failed to report the conviction and accordingly is in violation of
NRS 630.306(12).

13. By reason of the foregoing, Dr. Arcotta is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided NRS 630.352.

Count IV

14. NRS 630.304(1) provides that obtaining, maintaining, or renewing a license to
practice medicine by bribery, fraud or misrepresentation or by any false, misleading, inaccurate or
incomplete statement is grounds for initiating disciplinary action against a physician.

15. Dr. Arcotta answered negatively to question #6 on her renewal application
regarding whether she had been arrested or convicted in the time period specified within the
application, which is a false, misleading and inaccurate statement and accordingly, Dr. Arcotta is
in violation of NRS 630.304(1).

16. By reason of the foregoing, Dr. Arcotta is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided NRS 630.352.
