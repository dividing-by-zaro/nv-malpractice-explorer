COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, composed
at the time of filing was approved by Charles N. Held, M.D., Theodore Berndt, M.D.,
Ms. Valerie Clark, BSN, RHU, LUTCF, having a reasonable basis to believe that
Romulado Aragon, M.D., hereinafter referred to as "Respondent," has violated the provisions of
NRS Chapter 630, hereby issues its formal Complaint, stating the Investigative Committee's
charges and allegations, as follows: |

1. Respondent is currently licensed in active status (License No. 9214), and was so
licensed on November 4, 1999 by the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners pursuant to the
provisions of Chapter 630 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

2. Respondent practices primarily care and urgent care medicine in the Las Vegas
area. Between August 2008 and May 2011, Respondent practiced at a location which housed

FirstMed Urgent Care as well as Prime Care Medical Services, Respondent’s private practice.

3. Respondent served as medical director for FirstMed Urgent Care between August
2008 and February 2010.
4, While acting as medical director of FirstMed Urgent Care, Respondent pre-

signed/pre-stamped blank prescriptions. These pre-signed blank prescriptions were used by

5. Respondent also pre-signed physical examination forms for such uses as pre-

Count I

6. NRS 630.304(4) provides that signing a blank prescription form is grounds for
initiating discipline against a licensee.

7. Respondent has indicated that it has been his practice to stamp and/or sign blank
prescriptions in his practice and accordingly is in violation of NRS 630.304(4).

8. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NRS 630.352.

Count It

9. NRS 630.305(1)(e) provides that aiding, assisting, employing or advising, directly
or indirectly, any unlicensed person to engage in the practice of medicine contrary to the
provisions of [Chapter 630] or regulations of the Board is grounds for initiating discipline against
a licensee.

10. | Respondent allowed unlicensed nurse practitioner students/externs to engage in the
practice of medicine when he allowed them to use pre-signed blank physical examination forms
and pre-signed blank prescriptions during times when he was present at FirstMed Urgent Care and
at times when he was absent from the premises and accordingly is in violation of
NRS 630.305(1)(€).

11. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NRS 630.352.

Count Ill

12. NRS 630.306(2)(a) provides that engaging in any conduct which is intended to
deceive is grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

13. Respondent allowed unlicensed individuals to complete pre-signed physical
examination forms indicating that Respondent has examined the patient when he had not
examined the patient or was even present at the time of the examination.

14. Respondent also allowed unlicensed individuals to complete pre-signed blank
prescriptions.

15. | Respondent’s use of blank, pre-signed prescriptions and physical examination
forms is conduct which is deceptive and accordingly Respondent is in violation of
NRS 630.306(2)(a).

16. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NRS 630.352.

Count IV

17. NRS 630.306(2)(b) provides that engaging in conduct which the Board has
determined is a violation of the standards of practice established by regulation of the Board is
grounds for initiating disciplinary action against a licensee.

18. Section 630.230(1)(j) of the Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) provides that
failure to provide adequate supervision of an advanced practitioner of medicine is prohibited
standard of practice.

19. NAC 630.490(9)(a) provides that a collaborating physician shall ensure that an
advanced practitioner of nursing does not use pre-signed prescriptions.

20. The advanced practitioners of nursing that Respondent collaborated with and/or
continues to collaborate with have been allowed and directed to utilize pre-signed or pre-stamped
prescriptions pads in the Respondent’s practice.

21. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NRS 630.352.

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

1, That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners give Respondent notice of the
charges herein against him and give him notice that he may file an answer to the Complaint herein
as set forth in Section 630.339 of the Nevada Revised Statutes within twenty (20) days of service
of the Complaint.

2. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners set a time and place for a
formal hearing after holding an Early Case Conference pursuant to NRS 630.339(3);

3. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners determine what sanctions it
determines to impose if it determines there has been a violation or violations of the Medical
Practice Act (Nevada Revised Statutes Chapter 630) committed by Respondent; and

4. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners make, issue and serve on
Respondent its findings of facts, conclusions of law and order, in writing, that includes the
sanctions imposed; and

5. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners take such other and further

action as may be just and proper in these premises.
