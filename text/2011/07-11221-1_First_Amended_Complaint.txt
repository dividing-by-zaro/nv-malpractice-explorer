FIRST AMENDED COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee of the Board of Medical Examiners of the state of Nevada,
composed, at the time the filing of the First Amended Complaint was approved, of
Benjamin J. Rodriguez, M.D., Beverly A. Neyland, M.D., and Mr. Van V. Heffner, and by and
through Lyn E. Beggs, General Counsel for the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, having
a reasonable basis to believe that Robert Feingold, M.D., hereinafter referred to as "Dr. Feingold,"
has violated the provisions of NRS Chapter 630, hereby issues its formal First Amended
Complaint, stating the Investigative Committee's charges and allegations, as follows:

1, Dr. Feingold is currently licensed in active status (License No. 7916), and was
originally licensed on July 30, 1996, by the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, pursuant to
the provisions of Chapter 630 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

2. The true identities of the patients at issue in the First Amended Complaint are not
disclosed to protect their privacy; however, their identities are disclosed in the Amended Patient
Designation served on Dr. Feingold along with a copy of this First Amended Complaint.

3, Dr. Feingold ran a weight loss clinic, Medical Reducing, Inc. in Las Vegas, Nevada
at all times alleged herein. Medical Reducing, Inc. has an on-site pharmacy, Medical Reducing
Pharmacy. Dr. Feingold also provided weight loss treatment for patients through Focus Medical

Group.

Count I

80. Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.306(2)(b) provides that conduct which is a
violation of the standards of practice established by regulation of the Board is grounds for
discipline.

81. Dr. Feingold violated Nevada Administrative Code 630.205(1), a standard of
practice, in his prescribing of appetite suppressants for Patients A-L, in that he failed to prescribe
them as part of a program of medical treatment that included dietary restrictions, modification of
behavior and exercise.

82. Dr. Feingold’s failure to comply with NAC 630.205(1) thus constitutes a violation
of Section 630.306(2)(b) of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

83. By reason of the foregoing, Dr. Feingold is subject to discipline by the Nevada
State Board of Medical Examiners as provided in Section 630.352 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

Count Il

84. Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.306(2)(b) provides that conduct which is a
violation of the standards of practice established by regulation of the Board is grounds for
discipline.

85. Dr. Feingold failed to comply with Nevada Administrative Code 630.205(1), a
standard of practice, in his prescribing of appetite suppressants for patients E, F, I and L, when he
prescribed appetite suppressants to these patients when such a prescription was not medically
indicated as set forth in NAC 630.205(1)(a) or NAC 630.205(1)(b).

86. Dr. Feingold’s failure to comply with NAC 630.205 thus constitutes a violation of
Section 630.306(2)(b) of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

violation of the standards of practice established by regulation of the Board is grounds for
discipline.

89. Dr. Feingold failed to comply with Nevada Administrative Code 630.205(4), a
standard of practice, in his prescribing of appetite suppressants for Patients A-L, in that he failed
to perform adequate medical histories and physicals or conduct adequate studies to determine
whether any contraindications to the use of appetite suppressants existed.

90. Dr. Feingold’s failure to comply with NAC 630.205 thus constitutes a violation of
Section 630.306(2)(b) of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

91. By reason of the foregoing, Dr. Feingold is subject to discipline by the Nevada
State Board of Medical Examiners as provided in Section 630.352 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

Count IV

92. Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.3062(1) provides that the failure to maintain
timely, legible, accurate and complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care
of a patient is grounds for discipline.

93. Dr. Feingold’s records regarding Patients A-L are practically illegible, contain
minimal information regarding the diagnosis, treatment and care of the patients and are not
accurate and complete records.

94. By reason of the foregoing, Dr. Feingold is subject to discipline by the Nevada
State Board of Medical Examiners as provided in Section 630.352 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

Count VI

for discipline.

101. Nevada Administrative Code Section 630.040 defines malpractice as the failure of

103. Dr. Feingold’s failure to provide Patient F with the accepted standard of care
constitutes malpractice as defined by Section 630.040 of the Nevada Administrative Code, and
thus he has violated Section 630.301(4) of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

104. By reason of the foregoing, Dr. Feingold is subject to discipline by the Nevada
State Board of Medical Examiners as provided in Section 630.352 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.
