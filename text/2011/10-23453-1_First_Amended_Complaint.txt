FIRST AMENDED COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, composed
at the time filing was approved of Charles N. Held, M.D., Theodore Berndt, M.D. and
Ms. Valerie Clark, BSN, RHU, LUTCF, having a reasonable basis to believe that Anthony Cruz,
C.R.T., hereinafter referred to as Mr. Cruz, has violated the provisions of NRS Chapter 630,
hereby issues its formal Complaint, stating the Investigative Committee's charges and allegations,
as follows:

l, Mr. Cruz is licensed to practice respiratory care in the state of Nevada (License No.
RC598), and was originally licensed on December 31, 2001 by the Nevada State Board of Medical
Examiners pursuant to the provisions of Chapter 630 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

2. On August 3, 2010 Mr. Cruz was working at Summerlin Hospital and was reported
to be exhibiting signs of impairment. Mr. Cruz was requested to submit to a ten-panel urine drug
screen and he agreed to do so.

3. Results of the drug screen were forwarded to Summerlin Hospital a few days later
and showed that Mr. Cruz was positive for methamphetamine at the time of the test.

4, On August 9, 2010, Mr. Cruz was terminated from Summerlin Hospital.

5. On August 19, 2010, the Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of
Medical Examiners issued its Order summarily suspending the license to practice respiratory care
of Mr. Cruz pursuant to NRS 630.326(1).

6. The license to practice respiratory care of Mr. Cruz remains suspended at the time
of the filing of this Complaint.

Count I

7. NAC 630.540(9) provides that rendering respiratory care to a patient while under
the influence of alcohol or any controlled substance or in any impaired mental or physical
condition is grounds for the initiation of a disciplinary action against a respiratory therapist.

8. Mr. Cruz exhibited signs of impairment and subsequent to urine drug test was
found to have been under the influence of methamphetamine while at work at Summerlin hospital
where he provided respiratory care to patients on August 3, 2010.

9. By reason of the foregoing, Mr. Cruz is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NAC 630.555.

Count If

10. NAC 630.540(23) provides that violation of a provision of NRS 630.301 through
NRS 630.3065 is grounds for the initiation of a disciplinary action against a respiratory therapist.

11. NRS 630.306(1) provides that inability to practice medicine with reasonable skill
and safety because of illness, a mental or physical condition or the use of alcohol, narcotics or any
other substance is grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

12... Mr. Cruz was found to have been under the influence of methamphetamine, an
illegal drug while at work at Summerlin Hospital and therefore was unable to practice respiratory
care with reasonable skill and safety due to said drug usage.

13. By reason of the foregoing, Mr. Cruz is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NAC 630.555.

Count Il

14. NAC 630.540(1) provides that willfully and intentionally making a false or
fraudulent statement when applying for or renewing a license is grounds for the initiation of
disciplinary action against a respiratory therapist.

15. Mr. Cruz was arrested in 1994 for domestic battery but failed to disclose the arrest
when he applied for licensure in 2001 and failed to disclose on any subsequent renewal
applications.

16. Mr. Cruz was arrested in 2005 for a misdemeanor DUI but failed to disclose the
arrest on his 2006 renewal application or any subsequent renewal applications.

17. By reason of the foregoing, Mr. Cruz is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NAC 630.555.

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

1. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners give Mr. Cruz notice of the
charges herein against him and give him notice that he may file an answer to the Complaint herein
as set forth in Section 630.339 of the Nevada Revised Statutes within twenty (20) days of service
of the Complaint.

2. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners set a time and place for a
formal hearing after holding an Early Case Conference pursuant to NRS 630.339(3);

3, That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners determine what sanctions it
determines to impose if it determines there has been a violation or violations of the Medical
Practice Act (Nevada Revised Statutes Chapter 630) committed by Mr. Cruz; and

4. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners make, issue and serve on
Mr. Cruz its findings of facts, conclusions of law and order, in writing, that includes the sanctions
imposed; and
