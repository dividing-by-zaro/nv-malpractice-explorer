COMPLAINT By: ———

The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, composed
at the time  ffiling owas approved oof Theodore 3B. Berndt, MOD.,
Ms. Valerie Clark, BSN, RHU, LUTCF, and Michael Fischer, M.D., having a reasonable basis to
believe that Gary Roberts, C.R.T., hereinafter referred to as Mr. Roberts, has violated the
provisions of NRS Chapter 630, hereby issues its formal Complaint, stating the Investigative
Committee's charges and allegations, as follows:

1. Mr. Roberts is licensed to practice respiratory care in the state of Nevada (License
No. RC36), and was originally licensed on November 14, 2001 by the Nevada State Board of
Medical Examiners pursuant to the provisions of Chapter 630 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

2. On August 5, 2011 Mr. Roberts was working at University Medical Center (UMC).
While on duty, staff attempted to page Mr. Roberts over an extended period of time. He was
discovered in a restroom in UMC incoherent and disoriented with slurred speech and red, watery
eyes.

3. Mr. Roberts was taken to the emergency department of UMC and did provide a
urine sample which was determined to be positive for opiates.

4, Mr. Roberts does not have any current prescriptions for any opiate.

5. In the restroom that Mr. Roberts was discovered in, a syringe and needle were
found in the trash container and a loose hypodermic needle was found on Mr. Roberts in the
emergency department which was not consistent with those used in his respiratory duties.

6. Mr. Roberts resigned his position at UMC before the investigation into the matter
was completed.

7. Mr. Roberts has admitted to having an issue with substance abuse and reports he is
seeking treatment.

8. On August 20, 2011, the Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of
Medical Examiners issued its Order summarily suspending the license to practice respiratory care
of Mr. Roberts pursuant to NRS 630.326(1).

9, The license to practice respiratory care of Mr. Roberts remains suspended at the
time of the filing of this Complaint.

Count I

10. NAC 630.540(9) provides that rendering respiratory care to a patient while under
the influence of alcohol or any controlled substance or in any impaired mental or physical
condition is grounds for the initiation of a disciplinary action against a respiratory therapist.

11. Mr. Roberts was found to be incoherent and exhibited signs of impairment and
subsequent to urine drug test was found to have been under the influence of opiates while at work
at UMC where he provided respiratory care to patients on August 5, 2011.

12. By reason of the foregoing, Mr. Roberts is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in NAC 630.555.

Count II

13. NAC 630.540(23) provides that violation of a provision of NRS 630.301 through
NRS 630.3065 is grounds for the initiation of a disciplinary action against a respiratory therapist.

14. | NRS 630.306(1) provides that inability to practice medicine with reasonable skill
and safety because of illness, a mental or physical condition or the use of alcohol, narcotics or any
other substance is grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

15. Mr. Roberts was found to have been under the influence of opiates for which he did

16. | Byreason of the foregoing, Mr. Roberts is subject to discipline by the Nevada State

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

1. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners give Mr. Roberts notice of the
charges herein against him and give him notice that he may file an answer to the Complaint herein
as set forth in Section 630.339 of the Nevada Revised Statutes within twenty (20) days of service
of the Complaint.

2. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners set a time and place for a
formal hearing after holding an Early Case Conference pursuant to NRS 630.339(3);

3, That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners determine what sanctions it
determines to impose if it determines there has been a violation or violations of the Medical
Practice Act (Nevada Revised Statutes Chapter 630) committed by Mr. Roberts; and

4. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners make, issue and serve on
Mr. Roberts its findings of facts, conclusions of law and order, in writing, that includes the
sanctions imposed; and

5. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners take such other and further

action as may be just and proper in these premises.
