COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, composed
at the time of filing of Theodore B. Berndt, M.D., Chairman, Valerie J. Clark, BSN, RHU,
LUTCF, Member, and Michael Fischer, M.D., Member, by and through Bradley O. Van Ry,
Deputy General Counsel, having a reasonable basis to believe that Rakesh M. Nathu, M.D.,
hereinafter referred to as “Respondent”, has violated the provisions of NRS Chapter 630, hereby
issues its formal Complaint, stating the Investigative Committee's charges and allegations, as
follows:

1. Respondent is currently licensed in active status (License No. 9337), and has been
so licensed since April 10, 2000 by the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners pursuant to the
provisions of Chapter 630 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

2. Sometime in May 2010, Respondent became the subject of a federal investigation
into his medical billing practices. As a result of the investigation, Respondent agreed to pay $5.7
million plus interest by settlement agreement on June 8, 2011.

Count I

3. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

4, Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.301(9) provides that the engaging in conduct
that brings the medical profession into disrepute is grounds for initiating discipline against a
licensee.

5. The investigation of Respondent, allegations against Respondent and entering into
a settlement agreement with the Department of Health and Human Services and the Office of the
Inspector General constitute conduct that brings the medical profession into disrepute.

6. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State

Board of Medical Examiners as provided in Section 630.352 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

Count II
7. All of the above allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if
fully set forth herein.
8. Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.3062(1) provides that the failure to maintain

timely, legible, accurate and complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care
of a patient is grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

9. Medicare's billing investigation of Respondent, allegations against Respondent and
the entering into a settlement agreement with the Department of Health and Human Services and the
Office of the Inspector General arise from and/or are related to incomplete and inaccurate medical
records of patients and the diagnosis, treatment and care of patients.

10. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in Section 630.352 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

1. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners give Respondent notice of the
charges herein against him and give him notice that he may file an answer to the Complaint herein
as set forth in Section 630.339(2) of the Nevada Revised Statutes within twenty (20) days of
service of the Complaint;

2. That the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners set a time and place for a
formal hearing after holding an Early Case Conference pursuant to NRS §630.339(3);
