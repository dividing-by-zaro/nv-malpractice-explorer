COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners ("Board"),
composed at the time of filing of Benj amin Rodriguez, M.D., Chairman,
Beverly A. Neyland. M.D., Member, and Donna A. Ruthe, Member, by and through
Bradley O. Van Ry, Deputy General Counsel, having a reasonable basis to believe that
Dianna Jean Thomas, M.D., hereinafter referred to as “Respondent”, has violated the provisions of
NRS Chapter 630, hereby issues its formal Complaint, stating the Investigative Committee's
charges and allegations, as follows:

1. Respondent is currently licensed in active status (License No. 7097), and has been
so licensed since June 11, 1994 by the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners pursuant to the
provisions of Chapter 630 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

2. Patient A was a six (6) year-old male at the time of the incidents in question. His true
identity is not disclosed to protect his privacy, but his identity is disclosed in the Patient
Designation served on Respondent along with a copy cf this Complaint.

3. Patient B was a sixteen (16) year old male at the time of the incidents in question. His
true identity is not disclosed to protect his privacy, but his identity is disclosed in the Patient
Designation served on Respondent along with a copy of this Complaint.

4, Patient C was a four (4) year old male at the time of the incidents in question. His
true identity is not disclosed to protect his privacy, but his identity is disclosed in the Patient
Designation served on Respondent along with a copy of this Complaint.

5. On June 30, 2011, the Investigative Committee issued an order to Respondent
pursuant to NRS §630.311(1) ordering her appearance before the committee to address the
pending investigations, her medical practice in general, her specialty, the community standard of
care and her complaint history with the Board. The appearance date and time was scheduled for
Tuesday, August 23, 2011 at 10:30 am. at the offices of the Board located at
1105 Terminal Way #301, Reno, NV 89502.

6. Service of the order was made by mailing a true copy -by the
US Postal Service Certified Mail on July 19, 2011 pursuant to NRS §630.244.

7. Acceptance of service was made on behalf of Respondent on July 19, 2011.

8. On August 23, 2011, Respondent did not appear before the
Investigative Committee as ordered.

9. As to Patients A, B and C, Respondent received numerous requests for the medical
files/records of each patient. At no time has Respondent made the medical records available for
inspection or copying as required by law.

10. Further, Respondent closed her medical practice and terminated patient care of the
three (3) aforementioned patients without providing adequate notice to those patients.

Count I

11. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

12. | Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.3065(2)(a) provides that the willful failure to
comply with an Order of a committee designated by the Board to investigate a complaint against a
physician is grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

13. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in Section 630.352 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

Count IT

14. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

15. Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.3062(4) provides that the failure to make the
medical records of a patient available for inspection and copying as provided in NRS 629.061 is
grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

16. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in Section 630.352 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

Count OF

17. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

18. | Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.304(7) provides that terminating the medical
care of a patient without adequate notice or without making other arrangements for the continued
care of a patient is grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

19. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in Section 630.352 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.

Count IV

20. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

21. Nevada Revised Statute Section 630.301(9) provides that the engaging in conduct
that brings the medical profession into disrepute is grounds for initiating discipline against a
licensee.

22. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Nevada State
Board of Medical Examiners as provided in Section 630.352 of the Nevada Revised Statutes.
