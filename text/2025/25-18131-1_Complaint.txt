COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee’ (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through lan J. Cumings, Senior Deputy General Counsel and attorney for the IC,
having a reasonable basis to believe that Dana Ralph Marks, M.D., (Respondent) violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC)
Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating the IC’s
charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a medical doctor holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 9358). Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on May 1, 2000.

2. On June 2, 2023, Respondent entered into a Stipulation and Order to Continue
Participation in a Substance Abuse Recovery and Monitoring Program (Order) with the IC. See
Exhibit 1.

3. Per the terms of the Order, Respondent was to remain in a substance abuse recovery
and monitoring program, and per the terms of his contract, was to refrain from any mood-altering
drugs, including alcohol, and that Respondent would be subject to a random monthly drug and

alcohol screening.

' The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, at the time this formal
‘Complaint was authorized for filing, was composed of Board members Chowdhury H. Ahsan, M.D., Ph.D., FACC, Ms.
Pamela J. Beal, and Irwin B. Simon, M.D., FACS

4, Onor about September 29, 2025, Respondent provided a hair sample to a laboratory
drug testing facility, which was reported on October 3, 2025. The results of the drug test were
positive for the substances amphetamine and methamphetamine in violation of the Stipulation and
Order.
COUNTI
NRS 630.3065(2)(a) - Knowing or Willful Failure to Comply with a Board Order

5. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by reference
as though fully set forth herein.

6 —_NRS 630.3065(2)(a) provides that the knowing or willful failure to comply with an
order of the Board constitutes grounds for initiating disciplinary action.

7. Respondent knowingly or willfully failed to comply with his Board Order to abstain
from the use of any mood-altering drugs, including alcohol, by testing positive for amphetamine
and methamphetamine on September 29, 2025, indicating the use of mood-altering drugs, in
violation of the Stipulation and Order

8. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent 1s subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT
NRS 630.306(1)(h) — Having an Alcohol or Substance Use Disorder

9. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by reference
as though fully set forth herein.

10. Having an alcohol or substance use disorder is grounds for disciplinary action
pursuant to NRS 630.306(1)(b)(2).

11. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent entered
into an agreement with the Board on June 2, 2023, to remain in substance abuse treatment for a
period of five (5) years. Per the terms of the Order, Respondent was to refrain from any mood-
altering drugs, including alcohol. On September 29, 2025, Respondent provided a hair sample to a
laboratory drug testing facility which demonstrated that Respondent received positive results for the

substances amphetamines and methamphetamines indicating use of mood-altering drugs.
