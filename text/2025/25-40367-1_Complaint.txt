COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners (Board),
by and through Ian J. Cumings, Senior Deputy General Counsel and attorney for the IC, having a
reasonable basis to believe that Kenny Blas Hanna, M.D. (Respondent) violated the provisions of|
Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630
(collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating the IC’s charges and
allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a medical doctor holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 14660). Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on January 23, 2013.

2. Respondent's specialty is listed “Surgery, Hand,” on the Board’s website.
Respondent holds an American Board of Medical Specialties (ABMS) certification in general
surgery Respondent does not hold any ABMS certification from an Orthopaedic Board in
orthopedic nor hand surgery.

4. Respondent completed a five (5) year general surgery residency which included one

5. Respondent was employed by Pledge Medical. During Respondent's employment

Respondent’s Treatment of Patient A

6. Patient A? was a forty-two (42) year-old female at the time of the events at issue.

7. On July 13, 2019, Patient A presented to Respondent with complaints of clicking
and popping due to a hyperextension injury of her right knee from a March 16, 2018, motor vehicle
accident. Respondent scheduled Patient A for a platelet-rich plasma (PRP) injection of the nght
knee and ordered a magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) study.

8. On July 27, 2019, Patient A underwent the ordered MRI study which showed no.
signs of a meniscal tear or ligamentous injury, no osteochondral defects, but noted intra-articular
fat pad edema.

9. On July 28, 2019, Respondent performed another PRP injection of Patient A’s nght
knee.

10. On September 20, 2019, during Patient A’s follow-up appointment, Respondent
noted no improvement of Patient A’s right knee following the two (2) PRP injections and scheduled
her for arthroscopic knee surgery. Respondent failed to document a physical examination Patient
A’s knee or document a discussion of the risks and benefits of the surgery with her during this.
appointment.

COUNT
NRS 630.3062(1)(a) - Failure to Maintain Complete Medical Records

23. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

24. NRS 630.3062(1)(a) provides that the “failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate
and complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient” constitute
grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

25. Respondent failed to maintain complete medical records relating to the diagnosis,
treatment and care of Patient A, by failing to correctly document an examination of Patient A’s knee
in addition to failing to discuss the risks and benefits of surgery on September 20, 2019.

26. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT IL
NRS 630.306(1)(e) - Practice Beyond Scope of License

27. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

28. NRS 630.306(1)(e) provides that practicing or offering to practice beyond the scope
permitted by law or performing services which the licensee knows or has reason to know that he or’
she is not competent to perform, or which are beyond the scope of his or her training constitutes
grounds for initiating disciplinary action.

29. As demonstrated by, but not limited to the above-outlined facts, Respondent’s
specialty is listed as General Surgery under NRS 630.160. Respondent is not a board-certified
orthopedic surgeon and has only received one (1) month of instruction in orthopedic surgery during
his general surgical residency and therefore lacks the requisite and proper training in orthopedic
surgery necessary to perform orthopedic surgical procedures that Patient A underwent. Similarly,

Respondent is aware that he did not attend an orthopedic residency program, however represents

COUNT IV
NRS 630,304(2) — False or Deceptive Advertising

31. _Allof the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein,

32. NRS 630.304(2) provides that advertising the practice of medicine in a false,
