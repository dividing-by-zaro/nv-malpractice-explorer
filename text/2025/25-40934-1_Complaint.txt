COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through William P. Shogren, Deputy General Counsel and attorney for the IC,
having a reasonable basis to believe that Victor Wei-Kwong Fong, M.D. (Respondent) violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC)
Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating the IC’s
charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a medical doctor holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 18945). Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on June 11, 2019, and has a specialty n radiology, diagnostic and
internal medicine.

2. Patient A? was a forty-nine (49) year-old female at the time of the events at issue.

3. Patient A underwent a mammogram and breast ultrasound on May 4, 2021, at
Steinberg Diagnostic Medical Imaging in Las Vegas, Nevada, At the time, Patient A’s clinical
history included complaints of a palpable painful area in the right breast and a family history of

breast cancer.

The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners, at the time this formal
Complaint was authorized for filing, was composed of Board members Bret W. Frey, M.D, Col. Enc D. Wade,
USAF (Ret.), and Carl N, Williams, Jr., M.D., FACS.

4. A radiologist interpreted the May 4, 2021, results, and noted a cluster of cysts in
the upper outer right breast measuring up to 2.3 cm, as well as a deeper adjacent cyst measuring
3.3 cm. The radiologist noted an impression of “[bJilateral breast cysts, right greater than left.
Overall increased. Recommend six-month follow-up mammogram and ultrasound given change.
BI-RADS 3, probably benign.”

5. On November 8, 2021, Patient A returned for a follow-up mammogram and
ultrasound (hereinafter, collectively referred to as “the imaging”).

6. Respondent was the radiologist who interpreted the imaging results of the
November 8, 2021, mammogram. Respondent noted that Patient A had a family history of breast
cancer stemming from her grandmother and aunts. Respondent also compared the imaging with
previous mammograms, including the mammogram from May 4, 2021.

7. Respondent noted “numerous cysts, largest was 4 cm in the right breast at 9:00.
Similar 2 cm group of cysts at the reported palpable area in the right breast.” Respondent
determined that the bilateral breast masses were “benign-appearing,” and that there was “no

evidence of suspicious masses, malignant-type micro-calcifications, or architectural distortions.”

Respondent noted an impression of “BI-RADS 2: Benigt

8. Despite Patient A’s family history of breast cancer, complaints of palpable pain,
and the progression in size of the cyst in Patient A’s right breast, Respondent did not recommend
immediate additional evaluation or otherwise note any immediate concerns or suspicious
abnormahities. Instead, Respondent recommended a follow-up in one (1) year.

9. Patient A later reported feeling increased pain in her right upper breast. On
December 20, 2021, Patient A underwent a biopsy of the cystic lesion in the right breast.
Pathology that same day revealed an invasive carcinoma, measuring at least 5.5 cm.

11. NRS 630,301(4) provides that malpractice of a physician is grounds for initiating
disciplinary action against a licensee.

12. NAC 630.040 defines malpractice as “the failure of a physician, in treating a
patient, to use the reasonable care, skill, or knowledge ordinarily used under similar
circumstances.”

13. As demonstrated by, but not limted to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent failed
to use the reasonable care, skill or knowledge ordinarily used under similar circumstances when
rendering medical services to Patient A, when he failed to acknowledge or note, in his
interpretation of Patient A’s November 8, 2021, imaging, any concerns or suspicious
abnormalities, including, but not limited to, the possibility of breast cancer.

14. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent 1s subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

1. That the Board give Respondent notice of the charges herein against her and give
her notice that she may file an answer to the Complaint herein as set forth in NRS 630.339(2)
within twenty (20) days of service of the Complaint;

2. That the Board set a time and place for a formal hearing after holding an Early
Case Conference pursuant to NRS 630.339(3);

3. That the Board determine what sanctions to impose if it determines there has been
a violation or violations of the Medical Practice Act committed by Respondent;

4, That the Board award fees and costs for the investigation and prosecution of this
case as outlined in NRS 622.400;

5. That the Board make, issue and serve on Respondent its findings of fact,
conclusions of law and order, in writing, that includes the sanctions imposed; and
