FINDINGS OF FACT

Respondent held a license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada issued by the Board
from December 20, 1999 (License No. 9250).

I.

On September 16, 2024, an Order of Summary Suspension of Respondent's license was
issued by the Investigative Committee of the Board (IC) and was served on Respondent in-person
at the address on file with the Board. Another copy of the Order was sent by Fed Ex Overnight
and by email, at Respondent's request, to his physical residence in Coto de Caza, California, and
to his email on file with the Board. A summary suspension hearing was held on October 18, 2024,
in front of the Hearing Officer in which Respondent participated.

On or about November 21, 2024, Respondent made a formal request to be heard at the
March 7, 2025, Board Meeting which was granted by the Board. At the Board Meeting
Respondent requested modification of the Findings of Fact, Conclusions of Law and Order
(Order) issued by the Board in Case N No. 23-13009-1, the underlying matter in Case No.
24-13009-1. His request to modify the terms of the Order was denied by the Board and the
decision was reduced to writing and filed on March 20, 2025, and served to Respondent on that
same day.

The Hearing Officer issued an Order After Summary Suspension, filed December 2, 2024,
which recommended continuation of suspension of Respondent’s license. The IC filed a second
order after the Board Meeting on March 7, 2025, that order continued the suspension of
Respondent's license until the Board could adjudicate the instant matter.

mL.
On October 8, 2024, the Investigative Committee of the Board (IC) filed a formal

Complaint in Case No. 24-13009-1, alleging Respondent violated the Medical Practice Act. The

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW
L

The Board has jurisdiction over Respondent and the Complaint, and an adjudication of this

matter by the Board members as set forth herein is proper.
I.

Respondent was timely and properly served with the Complaint, and all notices and orders
in advance of the hearing and adjudication thereon, in accordance with NAC Chapter 630,
and NRS Chapters 630, 241, 622A and 233B, and all legal requirements of due process.

mm.

With respect to the allegations of the Complaint, the Board concludes that Respondent has
violated the Medical Practice Act, as alleged in the Complaint, as follows: one (1) count of
NRS 630,3065(2)(a), Knowing and Willful Failure to Comply with a Board Order. Accordingly,
Respondent is subject to discipline pursuant to NRS 630.352.

Iv.

The Board finds that, pursuant to NRS_ 622.400, recovery from Respondent of reasonable
attomeys’ fees and costs incurred by the Board as part of its investigation and disciplinary
proceedings against Respondent is appropriate. The Board has reviewed the Investigative
Committee’s Memorandum of Costs and Disbursements and Attorneys’ Fees; and the Board finds
them to be the actual fees and costs incurred by the Board as part of its investigative,

administrative and disciplinary proceedings against Respondent, and finds them to be reasonable
