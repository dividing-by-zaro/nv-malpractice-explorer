COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee’ (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Tan J. Cumings, Senior Deputy General Counsel and attorney for the IC,
having a reasonable basis to believe that David Michael Snipper, M.D., (Respondent) violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC)
Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating the IC’s
charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent was at all times relative to this Complaint a medical doctor holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada (License No. 6112), Respondent was
originally licensed by the Board on August 25, 1990, and specializes in anesthesiology.

2. Upon information and belief that Respondent was ingesting illegal drugs.
Therefore, Respondent was ordered to undergo an observed urine and hair drug test on
September 10, 2025. See Exhibit 1.

3. Respondent was served with a Board Order for Urine and Hair Sample (Order) on
September 9, 2025, pursuant to NRS 630.318 which required compliance by the end of business
September 10, 2025.

COUNTI
NRS 630.3065(2)(a) - Knowing or Willful Failure to Comply with a Board Order

8. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by reference
as though fully set forth herein.

9. NRS 630.3065(2)(a) provides that the knowing or willful failure to comply with an
order of the Board constitutes grounds for initiating disciplinary action.

10. Respondent knowingly or willfully failed to comply with the Board Order for urine
and hair collection to determine if he was taking illegal drugs.

11, By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

COUNT II
NRS 630.306(1)(j) - Failing to Timely Notify the Board of a Change of Permanent Address

12. Allof the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by reference
as though fully set forth herein.

13. NAC 630.540(23) provides that violating any provision that would subject a
practitioner of medicine to discipline pursuant to NRS 630.301 to 630.3065, inclusive, or
NAC 630,230, is grounds for disciplinary action against a physician.

14, NRS 630.306(1)(j) provides that failing to comply with the requirements of|
NRS 630.254 is grounds for initiating disciplinary action.
