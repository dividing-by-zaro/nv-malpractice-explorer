COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee! (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), by and through Sarah A. Bradley, Esq., Deputy Executive Director, and attorney for the IC,
having a reasonable basis to believe that Ayman Nicolas Awad, M.D. (Respondent) violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC)
Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its Complaint, stating the IC’s
charges and allegations as follows:

1. Respondent is a medical doctor holding a license to practice medicine in the State of
Nevada (License No. 8060). Respondent was originally licensed by the Board on
December 16, 1996.

2. On November 27, 2017, Respondent provided a self-report to the Board indicating that
he was arrested by the Lake Havasu City Police Department and charged with three felony counts.

3. On December 18, 2018, in the State of Arizona, Respondent pled guilty to
Surreptitious Photography, a Class 5 Felony.

4, Respondent was placed on probation for three years, ordered to serve 90 days jail time
in the Mohave County Jail, ordered to pay a $200 monthly probation services fee to the Clerk of the

Superior Court of Mohave County starting on January 1, 2019, due on the first day of each month

5. In lieu of a fine, the Court ordered that Respondent contribute $25,000 to five charity

6. Respondent is licensed in the State of Arizona under License No. 20281 for the
practice of allopathic medicine.

7. On August 6, 2019, Respondent entered into a settlement agreement with the Arizona
Medical Board regarding the above-referenced criminal matter.

8. As part of his agreement with the Arizona Medical Board, Respondent was evaluated
by a facility approved by the Arizona Medical Board related to his fitness for duty.

9. This evaluation indicated that Respondent was safe to continue practicing medicine
with completion of intensive, in-person continuing medical education (CME) courses in maintaining
professional boundaries.

10. The Arizona Medical Board found that Respondent’s conduct in the criminal matter
constituted unprofessional conduct.

LL. The Arizona Medical Board issued Respondent a Letter of Reprimand and placed him
on probation for two years.

12, As part of his agreement with the Arizona Medical Board, Respondent agreed to
complete the pre-course and in-person CME course portion of the Professional Boundaries and Ethics:
Extended Edition program offered by PBI, Inc. within six months. Respondent also agreed to
complete the post-course Maintenance and Accountability Seminars. Respondent agreed to provide
proof of completion of the CME, including participation in all seminars required by the program.

13. On July 9, 2020, the Arizona Medical Board found that Respondent had met the terms
of his probation and voted to terminate his probation.
