COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners (Board)
hereby issues this formal Complaint (Complamt) against Harish S. Hosalkar, M.D. (Respondent), a
licensed physician in Nevada. After investigating this matter, the IC' has a reasonable basis to
believe that Respondent has violated provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and
Nevada Admunistrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act). The IC
alleges the following facts:

1. Respondent was at all times relevant to this Complaint a physisican holding an
active license to practice medicine in the State of Nevada. His license was onginally issued on
March 5, 2014, and is currently licensed in active status (License No. 15200).

2. On November 16, 2018, the California Medical Board issued a Stipulated
Settlement and Disciplinary Order (Order). This Order disciplined Respondent with a stayed
revocation of his California license to practice medicine and placed him on probation for three (3)
years. Additionally, the Order stated that Respondent would take educational courses for each
year of his probation, and these courses shall include, but not be limited to, research protocols,
principle investigators’ roles and responsibilities, the Institutional Review Board’s (IRB) protocols
and processes and ethics in research.

3. Respondent did not report the Order to the Board within 30 days following its
issuance to Respondent.
Count
NRS 630.301(3) (Disciplinary Action by Another State Medical Board)

4. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

5. Pursuant to NRS 630.301(3), any disciplinary action, including, without limitation,
the revocation, suspension, modification or limitation of a license to practice any type of
medicine, taken by another state, among other entities, is grounds for initiating disciplinary action
against a licensee.

6. The California Medical Board Order constitutes disciplinary action by another state
entity.

7. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

Count IT
NRS 630.306(1)(k) (Failure to Report Within 30 Days)

8. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

9 NRS 630.306(1)(k) provides that failure to report in writing, within 30 days, to the
Board any disciplinary action taken against the licensee by another state is grounds for initiating
disciplinary action against a licensee

10. Respondent violated NRS 630.306(1)(k) when he failed to notify the Board, in
writing, within 30 days, of the California Medical Board’s Order of November 16, 2018.

11. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board of as
provided in NRS 630.352.
