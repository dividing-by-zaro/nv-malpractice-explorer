The Investigative Committee of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners (Board),
composed at the time of filing of Thomas’ B. Berndt, M.D., Chairman,

having a reasonable basis to believe that Thomas Abdella, M.D. (Respondent), violated the
provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code
Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its formal Complaint, stating
the Investigative Committee’s charges and allegations as follows:

lL. Respondent is currently licensed in active status (License No. 7589), and has been
so licensed since October 12, 1995 by the Board pursuant to the provisions of the
Medical Practice Act.

2. Patient A was a forty-three (43)-year-old female at the time of the incident in
question. Her true identity is not disclosed in this First Amended Complaint to protect her
identity, but her identity was disclosed in the Patient Designation served on Respondent in the
initial Complaint.

3. Patient A was admitted to the hospital on or about June 17, 2005 where she
remained until June 20, 2005.

4. During Patient A’s four (4)-day hospital stay, Respondent made four (4) written
entries in the patient’s chart. Three (3) of the four (4) entries were untimed. All four (4) entries
were illegible, either in part or in whole, and are incomplete.

COUNT I
(Medical Records Violation)

5. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.
6. NRS 630.3062(1) provides that the failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate and

complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient is grounds for
initiating discipline against a licensee.

7. Respondent failed to maintain accurate and/or complete medical records relating to
the diagnosis, treatment and care of Patient A when he wrote incomplete, untimed and/or illegible
entries in Patient A’s chart.

8. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

l. That the Board gives Respondent notice of the charges herein against him and give
him notice that he may file an answer to the First Amended Complaint herein as set forth in
NRS 630.339(2) within twenty (20) days of service of the First Amended Complaint;

2. That the Board sets a time and place for a formal hearing after holding an
Early Case Conference pursuant to NRS 630.339(3);

3. That the Board determines the sanctions it will impose if it finds Respondent
violated the Medical Practice Act;

4. That the Board makes, issues and serves on Respondent, in writing, its findings of
facts, conclusions of law and order, which shall include the sanctions imposed; and
