COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), composed at the time of filing the formal Complaint of Benjamin J. Rodriguez, M.D.,
Chairman, Beverly A. Neyland, M.D., Member, and Donna A. Ruthe, Member, by and through
Erin L. Albright, Esq., Board General Counsel and attorney for the IC, having a reasonable basis
to believe that Navneet N. Sharda, M.D. (Respondent), violated the provisions of Nevada Revised
Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630 (collectively,
the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its formal Complaint, stating the IC’s charges and
allegations as follows:

l. Respondent is currently licensed in active status (License No. 8200), and has been
so licensed by the Board since June 10, 1997, pursuant to the provisions of the Medical Practice
Act.

2. Respondent and his practice, Cancer Care Center, were investigated by the
Department of Health and Human Services Office of the Inspector General for Medicare (OIG)
for possible billing fraud.

3. During its investigation of Respondent and his practice, OIG discovered that
Respondent’s patient billings and patient medical records submitted to Medicare were not accurate
and/or complete as required by Nevada law.

4, The OIG investigation culminated in a settlement agreement executed by

Respondent on September 8, 2012.

COUNT I
(Medical Records Violation)
5. All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

6. NRS 630.3062(1) provides that the failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate and
complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient is grounds for
initiating discipline against a licensee.

7. OIG’s billing investigation of Respondent arises from and/or is related to

Respondent’s incomplete and/or inaccurate medical records for multiple patients.

8. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.
COUNT I
(Disrepute)
9. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by

reference as though fully set forth herein.

10. NRS 630.301(9) provides that the engaging in conduct that brings the medical
profession into disrepute is grounds for initiating discipline against a licensee.

11. Respondent’s aforementioned activities constitute conduct that brings the medical
profession into disrepute.

12. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352. .

COUNT III
(Billing)

13. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by

reference as though fully set forth herein.

14. NRS 630.305(1)(d) provides that the charging for visits to the physician’s office
which did not occur or for services which were not rendered is grounds for initiating discipline
against a licensee.

15. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, the patient billing
records reviewed by OIG during its investigation demonstrate that Respondent charged for visits
to his office that did not occur and/or charged for services that were not rendered.

16. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

WHEREFORE, the Investigative Committee prays:

1. That the Board give Respondent notice of the charges herein against him and give
him notice that he may file an answer to the Complaint herein as set forth in NRS 630.339(2)
within twenty (20) days of service of the Complaint;

2. That the Board set a time and place for a formal hearing after holding an
Early Case Conference pursuant to NRS 630.339(3);

3. That the Board determine the sanctions it will impose if it finds Respondent
violated the Medical Practice Act;

4, That the Board make, issue and serve on Respondent, in writing, its findings of
fact, conclusions of law and order, which shall include the sanctions imposed; and

5. That the Board take such other and further action as may be just and proper in these

premises.
