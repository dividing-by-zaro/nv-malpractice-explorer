COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), comprised of, at the time of filing of this Complaint,
Theodore B. Berndt, M.D., Chairman, Valerie J. Clark, BSN, RHU, LUTCF, Member, and
Michael J. Fischer, M.D., Member, by and through Bradley O. Van Ry, Esq., Board General
Counsel and attorney for the IC, having a reasonable basis to believe that David Lynn Packer,
M.D. (Respondent) has violated the provisions of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630
and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC) Chapter 630, collectively the Medical Practice Act,
hereby issues its formal Complaint, stating the IC’s charges and allegations, as follows:

1, Respondent is a physician licensed to practice medicine in the state of Nevada
(License Number 13014), and was originally licensed by the Board on January 28, 2009, pursuant
to the provisions of the Medical Practice Act.

2. Respondent was served with an Order for Evaluation by the IC on April 18, 2013,
setting an evaluation of Respondent for April 30, 2013, at 11:15 am., with Melissa Piasecki,
M.D., at her office located at 1135 Terminal Way, Suite 106, Reno, Nevada 89502.

3. Respondent failed to appear at the evaluation as ordered by the IC.

Count I

4, All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.

5. NRS 630.3065(2)(a) provides that the willful failure of a licensee to comply with
an order of the IC is grounds for initiating disciplinary action against a licensee.

6. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent's act
of not appearing at the IC-ordered evaluation constitutes the willful failure to comply with an
order of the IC.

7. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as

provided in NRS 630.352.

Count If
8. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by
reference as though fully set forth herein.
9. NRS 630.318(3) provides that the failure of a licensee to submit to an evaluation

when directed by the IC constitutes an admission of the charges against him.

10. NRS 630.306(13) provides that the failure to be found competent to practice
medicine as a result of an examination to determine medical competency is grounds for initiating
disciplinary action against a licensee.

11. | NRS 630.306(1) provides that the inability to practice medicine with reasonable
skill and safety because of illness and/or a mental or physical condition is grounds for initiating
disciplinary action against a licensee.

12. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent's
failure to appear at the IC-ordered evaluation constitutes an admission of his lack of medical
competency to practice medicine.

13. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.

WHEREFORE, the IC prays:

him notice that he may file an answer to the Complaint herein as set forth in NRS 630.339(2)
