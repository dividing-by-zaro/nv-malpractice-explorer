COMPLAINT

The Investigative Committee (IC) of the Nevada State Board of Medical Examiners
(Board), composed at the time of filing of Theodore B. Berndt, M.D., Chairman,
Valerie J. Clark, BSN, RHU, LUTCF, Member, and Michael J. Fischer, M.D., Member, by and
through Edward O, Cousineau, Esq., Deputy Executive Director and attorney for the IC, having a
reasonable basis to believe that Sean Phong-Quoc Su, M.D. (Respondent), violated the provisions
of Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) Chapter 630 and Nevada Administrative Code (NAC)
Chapter 630 (collectively, the Medical Practice Act), hereby issues its formal Complaint, stating
the IC’s charges and allegations as follows:

A. Status of Respondent’s Nevada Medical License

1. Respondent was licensed by the Board to practice medicine in the state of Nevada
on July 1, 1999 (License No. 9013).

2. On or about March 5, 2010, the status of Respondent’s license to practice medicine
in the state of Nevada was changed from active-restricted to active-probation. Respondent’s
license to practice medicine is presently in active-probation status.

C. Respondent’s Treatment of Patient B

5. Patient B was a thirty-seven (37)-year-old female at the commencement of the
incidents in question. Her true identity is not disclosed in this Complaint to protect her identity,

6. From on or about September 29, 2010 through December 2, 2011, Respondent
prescribed Patient B Phentermine, an appetite suppressant, on at least nine (9) separate visits. The
patient’s medical records do not demonstrate that the patient’s weight threatened her health.

7. The Nevada Prescription Monitoring Program shows that while Patient B was
being treated by Respondent she was also receiving prescription medication, including but not
limited to controlled substances, from eight (8) different physicians.

8. The progress notes contained in Patient B’s chart are inaccurate, illegible,
incomplete and/or untimely.

D. Respondent’s Treatment of Patient C

9. Patient C was a fifty-nine (59)-year-old female with a history of atrial fibrillation
and heart valve disease at the commencement of the incidents in question. Her true identity is not
disclosed in this Complaint to protect her identity, but her identity is disclosed in the Patient
Designation contemporaneously served on Respondent with this Complaint.

10. In November 2010, Respondent treated fatigue in Patient C with thyroid
medication. Respondent adjusted Patient C’s thyroid medication every three (3) weeks, which

failed to allow the thyroid medication to achieve a steady state.

COUNT I
(Medical Records Violation — Eleven Counts)

44. _ All of the allegations in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated as if fully set
forth herein.

45. | NRS 630.3062(1) provides that the failure to maintain timely, legible, accurate and
complete medical records relating to the diagnosis, treatment and care of a patient is grounds for
initiating discipline against a licensee. |

46. Respondent failed to maintain accurate and/or complete medical records relating to
the diagnosis, treatment and care of Patients A through K when he failed to maintain timely,
legible, accurate and complete medical records for Patients A through K.

47. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as

provided in NRS 630.352.
COUNT

(Malpractice — Eight Counts)

48. All of the allegations contained in the above paragraphs are hereby incorporated by

reference as though fully set forth herein.

49. — NRS 630.301(4) provides that malpractice of a physician is grounds for initiating
disciplinary action against a licensee.

50. | NAC 630.040 defines malpractice as the failure of a physician, in treating a patient,
to use the reasonable care, skill, or knowledge ordinarily used under similar circumstances.

51. As demonstrated by, but not limited to, the above-outlined facts, Respondent failed
to use reasonable care, skill or knowledge ordinarily used under similar circumstances when
treating Patient B, Patient C, Patient D, Patient E, Patient G, Patient H, Patient J and Patient K.

52. By reason of the foregoing, Respondent is subject to discipline by the Board as
provided in NRS 630.352.
